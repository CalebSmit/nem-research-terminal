### Arc Verdict

The arc is **Setup → Background Dump → Background Dump → Background Dump → Evidence → Evidence → Repeat → Evidence → Plateau → Stakes → Repeat → Plateau → Discovery.**

**The critical problem: Tabs 02–05 form a "dead zone" where the first-person story voice dies and a generic dashboard takes over.** The reader's emotional engagement, built by the excellent Tab 01, collapses across four consecutive tabs of context-setting before any new narrative beat lands. By the time the dashboard delivers real evidence (Tab 06–07), the emotional momentum is gone.

**The second problem: the best discovery moment (Tab 14, Credibility) is tab 14 of 14 — too late to drive conviction.** A judge scanning Tabs 1-5 may never reach it.

## B) The Memorable Sentence

### Candidate sentences:

1. **"The Market Is Pricing Newmont as If Gold Falls to $X. I Spent a Week Figuring Out Whether That's Right. It Isn't."** (Tab 01 header)
2. **"If you believe gold stays above $X, NEM is undervalued."** (Tab 15)
3. **"Zero major gold discoveries in 2023 AND 2024 — first time in 35 years of S&P Global data."** (Tab 12)
4. **"NEM beat production guidance in only 2 of 10 years. Average miss: -3.5%. But the trajectory tells the real story."** (Tab 14)

### Verdict

**Sentence #1 is the strongest.** It's personal, confrontational, and sets up a falsifiable claim. A judge will remember "the market says gold goes to $3,600 and this person spent a week proving it wrong."

**But it's only prominent in Tab 01.** The Verdict tab (15) does not repeat it in memorable form. The Verdict's hero section uses the same reverse DCF numbers but in a sterile, data-visualization format ("THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT INSIGHT") that lacks the first-person punch. The sentence that should ring in the judge's ears at the end is the one from the beginning — but it's buried under 14 tabs of intervening content.

**The sentence needs to bookend the dashboard.** It should appear in Tab 01 (beginning) AND in the Verdict (end), with the second occurrence adding what the 14 tabs proved.

## D) Does the Verdict Feel Like a Conclusion or Summary?

### Verdict: It's a **summary masquerading as a conclusion.**

Tab 15 (Verdict) contains:
1. A hero section restating the reverse DCF gap (already shown in Tabs 01, 02, and 06)
2. A "Final Verdict" box with BUY / Target / Upside (already shown in Tab 01 hero KPI strip and Tab 02 CMD)
3. A "Convergence of Evidence" section listing 4 layers — each of which just restates what the reader already saw
4. A bar chart showing 4 methods above current price
5. A conviction meter (visually nice but adds no new information)
6. A "Why NEM Over Alternatives" section (this is genuinely new and useful)
7. A closing paragraph that restates the thesis

**The problem:** The Verdict doesn't *conclude* — it doesn't tell the reader what to DO or what happens NEXT. It doesn't escalate stakes. It doesn't create urgency. A winning pitch ends with forward momentum: "Here's the catalyst calendar. Here's the kill criteria. Here's the first checkpoint — Q1 earnings on April 23. If AISC prints below $1,800, the thesis is confirmed."

The kill criteria exist but they're in Tab 01, not the Verdict. The catalyst dates exist but they're in Tab 11, not the Verdict. The Verdict should pull forward the most actionable elements from earlier tabs and end with a call to action, not a data summary.

## Summary of Recommendations

1. **Tabs 02-05: Break the dead zone.** Each needs a one-sentence "story beat" header that advances the argument, not just a data label. Tab 02 should open with the 3 non-consensus calls (already there, but buried). Tab 03 needs to answer "why gold won't revert to $3,600." Tab 04 needs to answer "what kind of company is this now vs. 2023?" Tab 05 needs to reveal that 3 mines carry the portfolio.

2. **Tab 08: Cut the verbatim risk repetitions.** Replace the "RISKS IDENTIFIED VIA ALTERNATIVE DATA" section with a FCF-impact quantification table that shows each risk's dollar impact on the target price. The reader already knows the narrative — give them the math they haven't seen.

3. **Tab 12: Cut the "WHAT THE BEARS HAVE RIGHT" box.** It repeats Tab 01's "WHAT ALMOST KILLED THE THESIS" section nearly verbatim. If it stays, each item must add a NEW layer (e.g., probability estimate, catalyst date for resolution, hedge strategy).

4. **Tab 15 (Verdict): Convert from summary to decision driver.** Pull forward kill criteria from Tab 01, catalyst dates from Tab 11, and the first checkpoint (April 23 Q1 earnings). End with forward momentum, not backward reflection.

5. **Elevate the memorable sentence.** The Tab 01 headline should reappear in the Verdict in a modified form that incorporates what the 14 tabs proved.

6. **Create a surprise moment around copper NAV.** The $12-15/share unpriced copper value is the single most differentiated data point vs. every other NEM analysis. Frame it as a reveal, not a footnote.

7. **Tab 02 reverse DCF banner: shrink or remove.** The full visual repeat of the implied gold gap drains the surprise for Tab 15's definitive version.
