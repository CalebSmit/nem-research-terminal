## 1. THE FATALITY — EXACT DETAILS

### Date and Timing
- **Incident date:** Wednesday, **February 4, 2026**, at approximately **4:00 PM local time**
- **Confirmed publicly:** Thursday, February 5, 2026 (Newmont statement, ABC News, NT Police)
- **Source:** [Newmont Official Media Statement, Feb 5 2026](https://www.newmont.com/investors/news-release/news-details/2026/Media-Statement-Tanami-Incident-2026-QUSGkX_YP8/default.aspx); [ABC News Australia, Feb 5 2026](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-02-05/nt-tanami-mine-newmont-confirms-worker-death-tragic-incident/106307980)

### Victim
- **Age:** 47 years old
- **Gender:** Male
- **Role:** Construction worker
- **Name:** Not publicly released
- **Employer:** Described as a Newmont workforce member; NT WorkSafe classified the death as occurring at "a construction site" on the mine — consistent with the Tanami Expansion 2 (TE2) shaft construction zone
- **Source:** [The Nightly (AAP/Lloyd Jones), Feb 5 2026](https://thenightly.com.au/australia/northern-territory/newmonts-tanami-gold-mine-construction-worker-47-killed-prompting-workplace-safety-investigations-c-21540583); [7News, Feb 5 2026](https://7news.com.au/news/man-killed-at-tanami-mine-in-northern-territory-as-machinery-malfunctioned-investigators-believe-c-21548089)

### Nature of Incident
- **Mechanism:** Suspected **winch failure during a lift** (lifting operation)
- NT WorkSafe statement (verbatim): *"Initial enquiries indicate a potential failure of a winch occurred during a lift as a contributing factor to the incident."*
- A "crime scene" was established; Coronial Investigation Unit and Crime Scene Examination Unit both attended
- **Source:** [Business & Human Rights Centre (aggregating ABC/NT Police/WorkSafe statements), Feb 5 2026](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/australia-a-mining-worker-died-at-tanami-gold-mine-site-raising-safety-concerns-and-investigations-are-underway-incl-co-comments/)

### Location
- Tanami mine site, Tanami Desert, Northern Territory, Australia
- Approximately **540 km north-west of Alice Springs** (~900 km south of Darwin)
- Underground mine extending more than **1.7 km below surface**, operational since **1983**
- 100% owned and operated by Newmont since 2002
- **Source:** [Xinhua, Feb 5 2026](https://english.news.cn/asiapacific/20260205/caf668efd8874a628527fe205ea6865e/c.html); [Yahoo News Australia, Feb 5 2026](https://au.news.yahoo.com/worker-killed-remote-gold-mine-032052000.html)

## 2. CONNECTION TO TANAMI EXPANSION 2 (TE2)

### Circumstantial Evidence — Strong but Not Explicitly Confirmed by Newmont
- NT WorkSafe stated the incident occurred at "**a construction site**" at the mine — not at a regular mining operation
- At the time of the incident, the only major active construction project at Tanami was **Tanami Expansion 2 (TE2)**, which was developing a 1.46 km-deep production shaft and hoisting system
- TE2 involves extensive winch, hoist, and shaft-related lifting operations (ABB Koepe friction winder, skip hoisting infrastructure)
- No news source or Newmont explicitly stated the victim was a TE2 construction worker, but the construction site location + winch failure mechanism is wholly consistent with shaft/hoist construction activities

### CEO Viljoen's Q4 2025 Earnings Call Confirmation (February 19, 2026)
On the Q4 2025 earnings call (15 days after the fatality), analyst Levi Spry asked about Tanami operations. CEO Viljoen's response (verbatim):

> *"The operational side of Tanami has been up and running within about four days after the incident. After the incident, we shut down the entire site. We made sure that all of our colleagues are looked after and that everybody is getting assistance through our EID process, and we wanted to make sure that people's focus is on operations so that it can be safe. Didn't want to distract their attention, so operations fully up and running. The project other than the shaft infrastructure, so **we stopped all work on the shaft infrastructure** but development for the ventilation underground infrastructure is back to normal to normal operations. The shaft infrastructure, we will start up as soon as we've completed our internal investigation and made sure that we understand the root cause of the incident, and make sure that it doesn't happen again."*

**This is decisive:** Viljoen's explicit reference to halting "shaft infrastructure" work after the incident confirms the fatality was connected to TE2 shaft construction.

- **Source:** [Fortune Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)

## 3. NT WORKSAFE INVESTIGATION — REGULATORY RESPONSE

### Investigation Status
- **NT WorkSafe formally commenced an inquiry** into the incident on or around February 4-5, 2026
- No public investigation number has been released
- Spokesperson statement (verbatim): *"Initial enquiries indicate a potential failure of a winch occurred during a lift as a contributing factor to the incident."*
- **Source:** [Business & Human Rights Centre, Feb 5 2026](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/australia-a-mining-worker-died-at-tanami-gold-mine-site-raising-safety-concerns-and-investigations-are-underway-incl-co-comments/)

### NT Police
- NT Police Force were notified around **4:00 PM Wednesday February 4**
- Officers from Yuendumu (~270 km from site) attended and **established a crime scene**
- Coronial Investigation Unit (CIU) and Crime Scene Examination Unit (CSEU) attended on February 5
- NT Police stated: *"Investigations are ongoing with members from the Coronial Investigation Unit and Crime Scene Examination Unit attending the site today."*
- **Source:** [Business & Human Rights Centre, Feb 5 2026](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/australia-a-mining-worker-died-at-tanami-gold-mine-site-raising-safety-concerns-and-investigations-are-underway-incl-co-comments/)

### Regulatory Significance
- The February 4 incident was reported as the **first mining fatality in Australia for 2026**
- [Work Safety Hub (weekly WHS round-up, Feb 3-9 2026)](https://www.worksafetyhub.com.au/blog/weekly-whs-round-up-3rd-to-9th-february-2026) classified it as "URGENT" and advised all mining/construction companies to "immediately review any similar lifting operations, check that winches, hoists and lifting gear are inspected and certified; reinforce exclusion zones"
- As of the research date (March 31, 2026), no public update from NT WorkSafe has been found on investigation conclusions, charges, or prosecution decisions

### Note on NT WorkSafe Institutional Context
- In February 2026, separate to this investigation, NT's Acting ICAC released a public statement finding "no improper conduct" but identified "shortcomings" in NT WorkSafe including "lack of adequate management and supervision of inspectors" — context relevant to the regulator's investigative capacity
- **Source:** [Inside State Government, Feb 11 2026](https://www.insidestategovernment.com.au/icac-investigation-of-nt-worksafe-finds-no-improper-conduct/)

## 4. IMPACT ON TANAMI OPERATIONS

### Immediate Shutdown
- **All activities at Tanami were suspended immediately** following the incident
- Newmont official statement (verbatim): *"All activities at the Tanami site have been suspended. Further information will be provided when it is appropriate to do so."*
- The mine employs approximately **1,800 fly-in, fly-out (FIFO) workers**
- **Source:** [Newmont Media Statement, Feb 5 2026](https://www.newmont.com/investors/news-release/news-details/2026/Media-Statement-Tanami-Incident-2026-QUSGkX_YP8/default.aspx)

### Operational Resumption Timeline
- CEO Viljoen confirmed on the Q4 2025 earnings call (February 19, 2026) that **mining operations resumed approximately 4 days after the incident** (i.e., by approximately February 8, 2026):

  > *"The operational side of Tanami has been up and running within about four days after the incident."*

- **TE2 shaft work remained halted** as of February 19, pending Newmont's internal investigation:

  > *"The shaft infrastructure, we will start up as soon as we've completed our internal investigation and made sure that we understand the root cause of the incident."*

- **Source:** [Fortune Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript, Feb 19 2026](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)

### Production Impact
- Tanami produced **408,000 oz of gold in 2024** and **268,000 oz in the first three quarters of 2025** (annualizing to ~357,000 oz/year for 2025)
- 2025 guidance for Tanami was **380,000 oz**
- Viljoen confirmed the incident and shaft stoppage were **not included as a production impact in 2026 guidance** — only "normal production at Tanami" was factored in:

  > *"What has been included is just our normal production at Tanami. That's what's been included in our guidance."*

- **Source:** [MarketWatch (Rhiannon Hoyle), Feb 5 2026](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/newmont-s-tanami-gold-mine-suspended-after-worker-death-aed84967); [Fortune earnings transcript, Feb 19 2026](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)

## 5. TANAMI EXPANSION 2 (TE2) — PROJECT STATUS AND TIMELINE RISK

### TE2 Overview
- **Project:** 1.46 km-deep production shaft + hoisting system + underground crush-and-hoist infrastructure
- **Capital cost:** A$1.7–1.8 billion (total project); Newmont had spent approximately A$1.3 billion by Feb 2026
- **Expected additional spend in 2026:** approximately A$330 million
- **Workforce (TE2 construction):** ~600 workers (400 on site at any one time); 98% Australian
- **Purpose:** Increase throughput from 2.7 Mtpa to 3.2 Mtpa; extend mine life to 2040+; add 150,000–200,000 oz/year gold production
- **Source:** [Territory Q magazine, Jan 2025](https://territoryq.com.au/tanamis-golden-future/); [International Mining, Dec 2024](https://im-mining.com/2024/12/06/newmonts-tanami-expansion-2-momentum-building/)

### Official Timeline (Pre-Incident)
| Milestone | Target Date | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Headframe and mechanical work completion | Late 2026 | Fortune Q4 2025 transcript |
| Commercial production | **Second half of 2027** | International Mining, Dec 2024 |
| Mine life extension | Until 2040+ | Territory Q, Jan 2025 |

- As of December 2024, the project was described as "on track for commercial production in the second half of 2027" with total capex A$1.7–1.8B
- As of February 2026 (Q4 earnings call), Viljoen confirmed: *"We are right on track to hit those targets"* and stated headframe/mechanical work expected to complete **late 2026**
- **Source:** [International Mining, Dec 2024](https://im-mining.com/2024/12/06/newmonts-tanami-expansion-2-momentum-building/); [Fortune Q4 2025 transcript, Feb 19 2026](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)

### Impact of Incident on TE2 Timeline
- As noted above, **all shaft infrastructure work was halted** pending Newmont's internal investigation into the winch failure
- Viljoen's statement indicates shaft equipping work was paused but "ventilation underground infrastructure" resumed
- Viljoen stated shaft work would resume "as soon as we've completed our internal investigation" — no specific timeframe given
- Newmont maintained its TE2 timeline guidance of H2 2027 commercial production as of February 19, 2026 — implying management believes the work stoppage does not materially delay the project
- **No external analyst or publication has publicly documented a formal delay to TE2 due to this incident as of March 2026**
- **Source:** [Fortune Q4 2025 transcript, Feb 19 2026](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)
