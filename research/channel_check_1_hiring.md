# Channel Check 1: NEM Hiring Activity
## Newmont Corporation (NYSE: NEM) — Workforce Signal Analysis
**Date of Research:** March 31, 2026  
**Analyst Purpose:** Alternative data channel check for equity research

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Newmont's hiring activity in early 2026 presents a mixed but net-positive signal. The company completed a significant workforce restructuring ("Project Catalyst") in November 2025, eliminating 3,552 positions (16% of workforce). However, what appears post-restructuring is a lean but active hiring posture: the careers portal shows ~121 open positions globally, LinkedIn tracks ~141 worldwide postings, and hiring is clearly targeted at strategic capital projects (Cadia Panel Caves, Lihir Nearshore Soil Barrier, Tanami Expansion 2) and digital/technology modernization. The presence of a Control Room Operator – AHS, Data Scientist, Lead Mining Digital Transformation, and Director of Digital (Peñasquito) roles signals ongoing commitment to technology investment even through cost discipline. Critically, the hiring freeze implied by the restructuring appears to be **over** — Newmont is now selectively filling operationally critical and project-driven roles, consistent with management's "trough year in 2026, growth return in 2027" narrative.

**Overall Signal: NEUTRAL-TO-BULLISH** — restructuring complete, selective growth hiring underway at key capital projects.

## SECTION 9: OVERALL INVESTMENT SIGNAL

**BULLISH — Confidence: Medium-High**

### Bullish Factors from Hiring Data:
1. **Restructuring is over.** No new severance filings or additional restructuring announcements. Hiring is live.
2. **Capital project execution signals are intact.** All three key growth projects (Cadia, Lihir Barrier, Tanami) show active hiring aligned with project milestones.
3. **Ahafo North is operational, not in distress.** Construction supervisor and geotech engineer are ongoing, indicating the ramp is progressing without setbacks.
4. **Technology investment continues through the cost discipline period.** Hiring a Data Scientist, Lead Digital Transformation, and AHS Control Room operators alongside cost cuts means technology is a protected investment — consistent with management's "Full Potential" program delivering $200M in value.
5. **Graduate program (2027 cohort) signals multi-year confidence.** Newmont is recruiting graduates across 6 disciplines specifically for Cadia, indicating long-term confidence in the panel cave project and the mine's future.

### Bearish Risks from Hiring Data:
1. **Lean hiring volume post-restructuring could indicate over-lean operations.** 121 open roles for a 22,200-person company is very low; could indicate cost discipline constraining operational readiness.
2. **Minimal visible Lihir construction-specific hiring.** The Nearshore Barrier is a $500–550M project starting now; absence of marine engineers, construction managers, or project engineers in direct Newmont hiring suggests significant reliance on contractors — execution risk if EPC delivery lags.
3. **Tanami commissioning roles not visible.** Tanami Expansion 2 is in shaft equipping/commissioning phase (H2 2026 target); no specific commissioning technician or commissioning engineer roles visible; could mean these are contractor-managed.

### Net Conclusion:
Hiring data corroborates management's "trough year leading to growth" narrative. Project Catalyst is definitively complete. Capital projects are live and hiring-supported. Technology investment is accelerating even within cost discipline. The hiring data does **not** raise any red flags inconsistent with company guidance and aligns with analyst upgrade rationale (new CEO, clear agenda, achievable guidance).
