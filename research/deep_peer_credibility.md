## SECTION 1: BARRICK GOLD (GOLD) — Guidance vs. Actuals 2020–2025

### Structural Notes
- Barrick reports attributable production (includes its proportionate share of joint ventures, notably 61.5% of Nevada Gold Mines with Newmont).
- Porgera (Papua New Guinea) was placed on care and maintenance from April 25, 2020 through December 22, 2023. Its re-opening contributed to 2024 volumes.
- Loulo-Gounkoto (Mali) was suspended in January 2025 following government action; original 2025 guidance of 3.15–3.50 Moz **explicitly excludes** Loulo production.
- No portfolio structural break comparable in magnitude to NEM's 2019 Goldcorp acquisition, though the 2019 Barrick–Randgold merger (completed Jan. 1, 2019) set the new baseline. All years 2020–2025 are on a comparable combined portfolio basis.

## SECTION 2: AGNICO EAGLE MINES (AEM) — Guidance vs. Actuals 2020–2025

### Structural Notes — Critical Flag: Kirkland Lake Merger
- **February 8, 2022:** AEM completed merger with Kirkland Lake Gold (KL). The combined entity added Detour Lake, Macassa, and Fosterville mines.
- **Pre-merger (2020–2021):** AEM was a ~1.7–2.1 Moz/year producer.
- **Post-merger (2022 onward):** Combined portfolio guided at ~3.2–3.5 Moz/year.
- **⚠️ Structural Break at 2022:** Direct comparison of pre-2022 vs. post-2022 guidance/actuals is NOT apples-to-apples. The table flags this explicitly.
- Additionally, in early 2021, AEM acquired Hope Bay from Agnico Eagle Canada (formerly Newmont's Hope Bay); this was ~56,000 oz in 2021 but the primary guidance excluded Hope Bay in 2021 per management practice.
- **2023:** AEM acquired full 100% of Canadian Malartic (previously 50/50 with Yamana Gold) on March 31, 2023. This further expanded the portfolio but was absorbed within the existing ~3.2–3.5 Moz guidance range.

**Note on 2020:** The initial point guidance of 1.875 Moz was set in February 2020. The miss vs. 1.737 Moz actual was entirely attributable to government-mandated COVID-19 mine suspensions (seven of eight operating mines halted in Q2 2020). This is a force majeure event, not a management credibility issue.

**Note on 2025 Guidance:** The initial 2025 guidance, as first issued in February 2024 (Q4 2023 release), was 3.40–3.60 Moz. This was revised down to 3.30–3.50 Moz in February 2025 (Q4 2024 release), primarily due to deferred processing of low-margin ore. For credibility scoring against the year's actual guidance given at the start of the year, the **operative guidance is 3.30–3.50 Moz** (guidance in effect at the start of 2025). This represents a mid-guidance revision, noted separately below.

**Pattern:** AEM has one of the strongest guidance track records in the gold sector. Post-merger (2022–2025), they have delivered within range every year, finishing above midpoint in three of four years. The 2020 miss was COVID-driven and broadly acknowledged as beyond management control.

## SECTION 3: NEM FY2026 AISC Guidance — Verification

### Confirmed Guidance

**Source:** Newmont Corporation Q4 and Full Year 2025 Earnings Release, February 19, 2026.

| Metric | 2026 Guidance |
|--------|---------------|
| **Gold By-Product AISC** | **$1,680 per ounce** (±5% range) |
| Gold Co-Product AISC | ~$1,935 per ounce (for reference) |
| Attributable Gold Production | ~5.3 million ounces (±5%) |
| Managed Portfolio AISC range | $1,650–$1,775/oz |
| Non-Managed Portfolio AISC | ~$1,400/oz |

**Answer to the specific question:** The FY2026 AISC guidance is a **single-point figure of $1,680/oz** on a gold by-product basis, subject to the company's standard ±5% range, implying an implicit range of approximately **$1,596–$1,764/oz**. The user's stated belief of "$1,620–$1,720/oz" represents a reasonable approximation of this ±5% band. There is no separate "from–to" range stated in the official release — just "$1,680 per ounce" as the point guidance.

**Context:** This compares to FY2025 actual gold by-product AISC of $1,358/oz — a year-over-year increase of ~24%, driven by lower production volumes (5.3 vs. 5.9 Moz), lower by-product output, and higher sustaining capex (primarily tailings facility work at Cadia and Boddington).
