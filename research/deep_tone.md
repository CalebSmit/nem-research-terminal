## SECTION A: ANALYST QUESTIONS ABOUT "CONSERVATIVE" GUIDANCE

### Q4 2025 Earnings Call — February 19, 2026 (CONFIRMED, VERBATIM)

**The most direct example found across all 2025-period calls:**

**Analyst:** Adam Baker, Macquarie
**Question (verbatim):**
> *"Hi, Natascha. I'm just wondering, from a corporate perspective, how you considered to lift your reserve and resource assumptions, noting that your resource, gold price assumption is now $2,000 an ounce and your reserves at $1,700 an ounce. You know, and why did you determine to do this? **Do you think this is still too conservative?** And, and I guess, how did the team land on that number? Thank you."*

**Response — Natascha Viljoen deferred to CFO Francois Hardy. Hardy's response (verbatim):**
> *"Yeah, thanks for your question, Adam. I think, we go through quite a rigorous process in terms of how we define our gold price assumptions. And we look at many, many different, market assumptions and direction. And the one we tend to align with reasonably closely is the three-year trailing average. And at the time of setting our 2026 gold price assumption for reserves, we were just above 80% of the three-year trailing average, which is typically we like to be in the low 80s, low to mid-80% of the three-year trailing average, and obviously, it's shot up since then. **We don't believe it's too conservative.** We have a rigorous process. If we look at our total portfolio, and we look at how we structure and look at our long-term mountains and the like. So at this stage, the 2,000 is the right number for us, but we continue to evaluate short-term opportunities and the like. And I'll just point to reminding you that the mine plan assumptions and the reserve and resource assumptions that we make are two different numbers that we optimize against. Thank you."*

- **Source:** [Fortune Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript, Feb 19 2026](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)

## SECTION B: RESERVE PRICE ASSUMPTION — EXACT QUOTE

### Context: Q4 2025 Call (February 19, 2026)
Newmont raised its reserve gold price assumption from $1,700/oz to **$2,000/oz** for 2025 reserves, and kept its 2026 resource assumption at $2,000/oz.

**Francois Hardy (Chief Technical Officer), Q4 2025 earnings call (verbatim):**
> *"We have increased our reserve price assumption for 2025 from $1,700 per ounce to $2,000 per ounce."*

And, in a separate statement (verbatim):
> *"Even with this increase, our reserve price assumption remains **conservative at more than 20% below the three-year trailing average** and well below spot."*

(At spot gold of ~$2,900–3,000/oz in early 2026, a $2,000 reserve price was ~30%+ below spot; the "three-year trailing average" referenced implies ~$2,500+/oz, making $2,000 approximately 20-25% below.)

- **Source:** [Fortune Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript, Feb 19 2026](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/)

## SECTION E: CEO LANGUAGE EVOLUTION — PALMER VS. VILJOEN

### Tom Palmer (Q1–Q3 2025: April to October 2025)

**Signature phrases and tone:**
- **Stabilization focus:** Palmer's language consistently emphasized "stabilizing" the portfolio following the Newcrest integration and divestitures. Example: *"second [priority] to stabilize our 11 managed operations"* (Q1 2025 opening)
- **Three-priority framework:** Repeated verbatim across Q1–Q3: *(1) Strengthen safety culture, (2) Stabilize 11 managed operations, (3) Execute on capital returns*
- **"On track" as mantra:** Used multiple times per call; rarely deviated from positive language
- **Confident but cautious tone on forward outlook:** When analysts pushed on guidance conservatism (Q2, Daniel Morgan), Palmer deflected rather than confirming or denying

**Characteristic Palmer quotes:**
- Q1: *"We remain firmly on track to meet our 2025 guidance, continuing on our journey towards creating the world's leading gold and copper portfolio for the benefit of our shareholders."*
- Q2 (on acquisitions): *"Our focus is internal and the best use of our capital is to buy back Newmont's stock."*
- Q3 (retirement announcement context): *"When I joined Newmont more than a decade ago, I could not have predicted the remarkable transformation our company would undertake."*

**Palmer's farewell tone (Q3 2025):** Reflective, legacy-focused; announced his retirement and Viljoen's elevation to CEO, calling the portfolio "the benchmark for responsible gold mining."

### Natascha Viljoen (Q4 2025 / FY2025 Call: February 19, 2026 — FIRST CALL AS CEO)

**Opening statement signaling continuity:**
> *"At the beginning of this year, I transitioned into my new role as Chief Executive Officer of Newmont, and I want to be clear that the priorities that guided me as Chief Operating Officer and that contributed to Newmont's success in 2025 remain firmly in place."*

**New five-priority framework** (replacing Palmer's three):
1. Ensuring safety remains the highest priority
2. Embedding efficiency, including cost and capital discipline
3. Demonstrating Newmont is the best owner/operator of its assets
4. Developing the highest return projects
5. Enhancing shareholder returns by improving per-share metrics

**Tone evolution — key differences from Palmer:**
| Dimension | Tom Palmer | Natascha Viljoen |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Safety framing | First of three priorities | Elevated; referenced Tanami tragedy personally and emotionally |
| Stability language | "Stabilize operations" (operative word) | Replaced with "continuous improvement" and "operational consistency" |
| Capital return emphasis | Third priority | Fifth priority (last), but with new enhanced framework (4% dividend increase + buybacks) |
| Forward language | "Journey" / "on track" | "Disciplined approach" / "decade-long foundation" / "enduring value" |
| Tone | Process-oriented, deliberate | Decisive, ownership-focused ("the priorities that guided me") |

**Viljoen opening remarks (verbatim):**
> *"Today marks my first full year results since formally taking on the role of CEO. A proud moment for me personally, but more importantly, as these results show, it's an exciting time to be part of Newmont."*

> *"2025 was a milestone year for Newmont as we achieved our full-year guidance, improved our operational performance and strengthened our financial position, resulting in record earnings and free cash flow on a quarterly and annual basis."*

**On the Tanami fatality (emotional register — notably personal):**
> *"We are devastated by the tragic loss of one of our colleagues recently at Tanami. Our thoughts are with their loved ones at this very difficult time. We maintain an unrelenting focus on a strong safety culture."*

(Compare to Palmer's typically clinical safety language: "We saw a notable decrease in the frequency of significant potential events" — Q1 2025)

**Closing remarks (verbatim):**
> *"2025 was a year of execution and follow-through as we achieved our full year guidance, finished the year strong, with a strong financial position, optimized our cost structure, advanced project capability, delivered meaningful exploration success, and returned capital to shareholders, reinforcing the solid foundation we have built and the potential of this organization."*

- **Source:** [Fortune Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript](https://fortune.com/company/newmont-mining/earnings/q4-2025/); [YouTube FY2025 Results Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7BgbahbU5og)
