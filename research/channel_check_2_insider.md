# Channel Check 2: Insider Trading Analysis

## Investigation
Analyzed 81 SEC Form 4 filings for Newmont Corporation (NEM) over the trailing 12 months (April 2025 - March 2026) using structured finance data.

## Key Findings

### ZERO Open Market Purchases
- Not a single NEM insider made an open-market purchase in the last 12 months
- This is notable given the stock rose from ~$51 to ~$108 during this period

### 21 Open Market Sales ($7.6M total)
Key sellers:
- **Bruce R. Brook** (Board Director): 8 sales totaling ~16,625 shares (~$1.17M) — systematic monthly selling pattern from May 2025 to Dec 2025
- **Peter Toth**: 5 sales totaling 15,000 shares (~$1.01M) — similar monthly pattern
- **Mark C. Rodgers**: 3 sales in Feb-Mar 2026 totaling 10,951 shares (~$1.39M)
- **David John Thornton**: 2 sales in Mar 2026 totaling 9,004 shares (~$1.10M) 
- **Thomas Ronald Palmer** (Former CEO): 1 sale Nov 2025, 5,000 shares @ $81.34 ($407K)
- **David James Fry**: 1 sale Mar 2026, 18,394 shares @ $111.45 ($2.05M) — largest single transaction

### Stock Awards & Tax Withholding
- 26 stock award grants (A-AWARD) — normal compensation
- 30 tax withholding transactions (F-INKIND) totaling 90,897 shares ($8.7M) — routine tax obligations on vested awards

### Pattern Analysis
- The selling is predominantly systematic/scheduled (Brook and Toth show identical monthly cadence)
- Palmer's single sale could be post-departure liquidation
- No cluster buying around the Q4 2025 earnings or 2026 guidance announcement
- CEO Natascha Viljoen: NO transactions at all — neither buying nor selling

## Investment Signal: NEUTRAL-TO-SLIGHTLY-BEARISH
The absence of insider buying during what management publicly describes as a "trough year" (when shares would theoretically be cheap) is a mild negative signal. However, most selling appears systematic rather than conviction-based. The former CEO sold only a small amount. CEO Viljoen's absence from both sides is inconclusive.

**Honest assessment:** This does NOT confirm the bull thesis the way insider buying would. The systematic selling pattern suggests insiders view shares as fairly valued at current levels, not deeply undervalued. This is an honest finding that challenges the bull case on the margin.

## Confidence: HIGH
