# Phase 2: Management Guidance Credibility Study
## Newmont Corporation (NEM) — Historical Guidance vs. Actual Results
### Prepared: March 31, 2026 | Gold Price Reference: ~$4,576/oz

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Newmont has a **mixed but improving** guidance credibility record over 2019–2025. The company consistently missed on production in its early years (particularly 2019–2021), largely due to COVID disruptions, the Goldcorp integration, and operational issues. Beginning in 2022, management became more conservative with initial guidance and improved delivery. AISC performance has been reliably within or slightly above original guidance. CapEx overruns are a systemic pattern, driven by inflationary pressure and major project investments.

**Key Findings:**
- Production beat rate (2020–2025): **0 of 6 years** (0%) — NEM has missed initial production guidance every single year in this window
- Average production miss magnitude (2020–2025): **−5.4%** overall; improving to **−3.1%** in recent 2022–2025 era
- AISC beat rate (2020–2025): **1 of 6** (17%) — only 2025 on by-product methodology
- CapEx discipline rate (actual ≤ guided): **3 of 6 years** (50%) — underruns in COVID years (2020, 2021) and 2025; overruns in 2022–2024

**Formula:** Incremental FCF = ΔProduction (Moz) × (Gold Price − AISC)

**At Base Case: 5.09 Moz actual production (vs. 5.26 Moz guided)**

- Net margin per oz = $4,576 − $1,680 = **$2,896/oz** (at guided AISC)
- **FCF Impact = −0.17 Moz × $2,896 = −$492M** (lost FCF vs. guidance case)

**At Conservative Case: 5.24 Moz production**
- ΔProduction = −0.02 Moz
- **FCF Impact = −$58M** (essentially neutral)

**At In-Line Case: 5.26 Moz production**
- **FCF Impact = $0** (guidance fully realized)

**At Upside: 5.37 Moz (if NEM beats guidance as it did in 2025)**

- Gold AISC cost: 5.26 Moz × $1,680 = **~$8.8B**
- Gold gross FCF contribution: **~$15.2B** — a phenomenal margin environment

## SECTION 8: QUALITATIVE ASSESSMENT OF GUIDANCE RISK

### Factors that could cause NEM to MISS 2026 guidance:
1. **Mine sequencing at Ahafo South** — management flagged this as a key production headwind in 2026
2. **Cadia Panel Cave transition** — complex cave mining ramp-up at grades lower than PC1; production drops ~40% in 2025–2026 before recovering
3. **Peñasquito grade variability** — sensitive to ore mix changes
4. **Cost inflation from gold price** — royalties and production taxes scale with gold price; at $4,576, royalty drag could be 20–40$/oz higher than guided
5. **Tanami Expansion 2 construction delays** — ongoing project execution risk

### Factors that could cause NEM to BEAT 2026 guidance:
1. **Ahafo North ramp-up** — commercial production declared Oct 2025; guided for 300 Koz/yr, upside if ramp faster
2. **Nevada Gold Mines outperformance** — NGM has consistently delivered in recent quarters
3. **By-product credits** — if copper/silver remain elevated, by-product AISC could come in below $1,680
4. **Lihir operational improvement** — management investing in sustainable mine plan; upside if earlier stabilization
5. **Portfolio simplification benefit** — non-core assets divested; remaining core portfolio is higher-margin

### Risk-Adjusted Assessment:
Given the **systemic production miss bias** in NEM's history (even in 2024, they missed by 50 Koz on 6,900 Koz guidance), and the complexity of the 2026 transition at Cadia, the **most likely outcome is delivery at or slightly below guidance** (within 2–3% miss). At $4,576 gold, even a modest 100 Koz miss costs ~$290M FCF — but at these gold prices, NEM's FCF generation is enormous regardless.

## SECTION 9: CONCLUSIONS & INVESTMENT IMPLICATIONS

### 1. NEM's Guidance Credibility Has Materially Improved Since 2023
The 2024 and 2025 fiscal years mark a structural improvement in guidance delivery. Natascha Viljoen's management team under the Tier 1 portfolio strategy is clearly setting more conservative initial guidance. The 2025 "beat" on production is essentially flat (−0.1 Moz or −0.2%), showing precise delivery.

### 2. AISC Guidance Remains the Key Risk
The company has consistently underestimated AISC, partly due to:
- Gold price-linked royalties (when gold rises above guidance assumption, royalties escalate)
- Inflationary pressures on labor, energy, consumables
- 2026 AISC guided at $1,680 (by-product) — achievable IF by-product credits remain high (requires copper at ~$4+/lb)

### 3. CapEx Has Normalized After COVID-Era Variability
Post-COVID, CapEx overruns of ~7–12% are the norm. The 2026 guidance of ~$3.35B (including capitalized interest) carries upside risk of ~$200–400M if tailings work and Tanami/Cadia projects face delays or cost escalation.

### 4. Peer Comparison Favors AEM for Guidance Reliability
Agnico Eagle consistently delivers tighter to guidance with smaller production misses. Barrick is the worst at guidance delivery among the three. NEM is catching up to AEM's standard but has more legacy baggage.

### 5. FCF Sensitivity at $4,576/oz Is Massive
At current gold prices, the spread between NEM's AISC ($1,680) and spot ($4,576) is an **extraordinary $2,896/oz margin**. Even with a 5% production miss, NEM generates ~$14.3B in gold FCF. The guidance credibility question matters less for absolute FCF (which will be record-setting regardless) and more for precise modeling of capital return capacity.

### 6. Implied 2026 Production Base Case: 5.15–5.26 Moz
Applying recent guidance discipline (2024–2025 avg miss of −0.45%) to the 5.26 Moz guidance implies **~5.24 Moz actual production** — essentially at guidance. The stock should be valued on the guidance figure with modest haircut.
