### Pre-2022 Average (Structural Shift Reference)
- **Annual average 2010–2021: ~473 tonnes**

- **The structural shift year is 2022**, which saw a 152% increase over 2021 (450t) and set the all-time modern record
- Context: The surge was driven by geopolitical risk from the Ukraine war/Russia sanctions, plus 40-year-high inflation — central banks began diversifying away from USD reserves dramatically faster
- Central banks have been **net buyers for 15 consecutive years** (since 2010), but the pace tripled starting in 2022

## 2. ESG-Mandated AUM

### The $35T+ Figure — Context and Nuance

The "$35 trillion" figure cited in many materials refers to **2020 data from the GSIA (Global Sustainable Investment Alliance)** before a major methodology change:

- **2020 GSIA report**: $35.3 trillion in global sustainable investment AUM (this is the origin of the "35T+" figure)
- **2022 GSIA report**: Figure dropped to **$30 trillion** due to a methodology change that halved US ESG AUM from ~$17T to ~$8T to exclude broad exclusion screens that were previously counted
- **2024 GSIA report**: Using Morningstar fund disclosure data (limited to legally disclosed assets only), finds **$16.7 trillion** in fund assets reporting R&SI approaches — but this is a definitional/scope reduction, not a market contraction
- **Bloomberg Intelligence (2024)**: Confirms ESG assets surpassed **$30 trillion in 2022** and projects **>$40 trillion by 2030** (>25% of projected $140T total AUM)

### Key Data Points for Annotation
- **Best defensible current figure**: ~$30–35T in broadly-defined ESG/sustainable AUM (2022–2024 range depending on definition)
- **Source for the "$35T+" claim**: GSIA 2020 Global Sustainable Investment Review; Bloomberg Intelligence references this as a floor
- **Projection**: Bloomberg Intelligence projects **>$40T by 2030**
- **PwC (2022)**: Projects ESG-related AUM to reach **$33.9T by 2026**, from $18.4T in 2021 (CAGR 12.9%)
- **Morgan Stanley (2024)**: Dedicated sustainable funds reached **$3.5T AUM** in H1 2024 (this is a narrower subset — pure ESG funds only, not broad mandates)

### Annotation Recommendation
Use: **"$30T+ in ESG-mandated assets globally (GSIA/Bloomberg Intelligence, 2022)"** — this is the most defensible cited figure. The $35T figure is the 2020 GSIA number; the methodology-adjusted 2022 figure is $30T. If using the pre-methodology-change convention, $35T (2020) is accurate.

## 3. Gold Mine Discovery Drought

### The Discovery Drought — Verified Data
- **For the first time in recorded history, 2023 and 2024 each saw zero major gold discoveries** (defined as deposits with 2M+ ounces of gold). This has never happened in two consecutive years in the modern era of gold exploration data.
- Source: S&P Global Market Intelligence annual analysis of major gold discoveries (confirmed in their 2025 update)
- **Between 1990 and 2024**: 353 major deposits identified globally, holding 3 billion ounces total
- **Since 2020**: Only 6 major discoveries total (27 Moz combined); average size 4.4 Moz vs. 7.7 Moz average from 2010–2019
- **None of the discoveries from the past 10 years rank among the 30 largest gold discoveries ever**
- **Peak discovery years were mid-1990s** (20–28 significant deposits annually); 2006 saw 22 discoveries; since 2010, mostly single digits; 2023–2024: zero

### The 17.8-Year Discovery-to-Production Timeline
- **Confirmed figure: 17.8 years** is the average lead time for mines that became operational **2020–2024** (discovery through to first production)
- Source: [S&P Global Market Intelligence, April 2025](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/from-6years-to-18years-the-increasing-trend-of-mine-lead-times): "The average lead time for mines continues to rise, reaching 17.8 years for those that became operational between 2020 and 2024. This duration is nearly three times longer than the lead time for mines that began operations from 1990 to 1999."
- Prior S&P Global study (April 2024): found **17.9 years** for mines operational 2020–2023 (essentially the same figure)
- Global average across all mines since 2002: ~15.5–16 years
- US average: ~19.1 years (second-longest globally after Zambia)

### Annotation for Dashboard
- **"Zero major gold discoveries in 2023 and 2024 — a historic first. With a 17.8-year average lead time from discovery to production (S&P Global), no discoveries today = no new supply through the late 2030s."**

### Sources
- [S&P Global — Mine Lead Times, April 2025](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/from-6years-to-18years-the-increasing-trend-of-mine-lead-times): 17.8 years for 2020–2024 mines
- [S&P Global — Gold Major Discoveries (2025 update)](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/new-finds-remain-scarce-despite-gold-from-major-discoveries-at-3-boz): "Notably, no major discoveries occurred in 2023–24"

## 4. NEM's Cadia Mine AISC

### The ~$400/oz Figure — Context and Sourcing

The "$400/oz" figure for Cadia AISC does **not** match current (2024–2025) reported costs. This figure appears to reference either:

**(a) Historical Newcrest-era by-product AISC (2019–2022 period):**
- In the March 2021 quarter, Cadia's AISC was **negative $160/oz** (a record low, due to high copper by-product credits)
- In FY2021 (Newcrest's annual report), Cadia achieved a record low annual AISC of **negative $109/oz**
- In Q4 2021 (Dec quarter), Cadia's AISC was **negative $19/oz**
- In Q2 2021 (Jun quarter), Cadia's AISC was **negative $377/oz** (lowest ever, due to record copper prices)
- In the 2019 period, Cadia AISC was roughly ~A$206/oz (approximately US$140–150/oz)

**(b) Projected PC1-2 cave life-of-mine AISC:**
- The 2021 Newcrest PC1-2 Pre-Feasibility Study projected an average AISC of **A$54/oz (US$41/oz)** over the 17-year PC1-2 cave life — an extraordinarily low figure driven by massive copper by-product credits

**(c) The most likely source of the "$400/oz" claim:**
- A blended or rounded figure cited during the 2022–2023 analyst period when Cadia (then Newcrest, before Newmont acquisition) was regularly posting AISC in the range of $300–$700/oz depending on copper prices
- Some analyst notes from 2022 cited Cadia AISC of approximately $400–600/oz as a representative figure

### CURRENT Cadia AISC (Post-Newmont Acquisition, 2024–2025)
- **2024 guidance (gold co-product)**: AISC ~**$1,150/oz** (from Newmont's Feb 2024 outlook)
- **2025 guidance**: Cadia gold AISC guided higher at ~**$1,950/oz** for gold co-product, as gold production is decreasing ~40% during the cave transition to PC2-3 (lower grades) and tailings capital spending increases
- **Cadia copper (2024)**: ~80,000 tonnes at AISC of ~$6,580/tonne

**Key context**: Cadia's historically very low AISC (and sometimes negative AISC) was because copper revenue was credited as a by-product against gold costs. When reported on a co-product basis (allocating costs proportionally to gold AND copper), AISC was always higher. Since the Newmont acquisition (Nov 2023) and the cave transition, Cadia's gold AISC has risen significantly.

### Global Average Gold AISC
- **Q3 2024 (World Gold Council)**: Global industry average AISC reached **$1,456/oz** (a record high at that time, up 4% QoQ, up 9% YoY)
- **~$1,400/oz** entering 2026 per Bunker Group analysis (with regional variation)
- **2024 US average**: $1,716/oz; **2024 Canada average**: $1,513/oz (S&P Global)
- **WisdomTree (2025)**: "Median AISC across gold miners barely scrapes past $1,600"
- **Newmont total portfolio 2024**: Gold AISC of ~$1,475/oz (Q3 2024 actuals); $1,400/oz full year 2024 guidance (Tier 1 Portfolio: ~$1,300/oz)
- **Newmont full year 2025**: Gold by-product AISC of **$1,358/oz** (co-product was higher at ~$1,566/oz in Q3 2025)

## Notes and Caveats

1. **Cadia $400/oz**: This figure likely originated from Newcrest-era historical reporting (2019–2022) when by-product copper credits frequently brought gold AISC below $500/oz, sometimes deeply negative. It is not representative of current Newmont-owned Cadia AISC. Recommend using either "historically negative to ~$400/oz AISC (Newcrest era, by-product)" or citing the PC1-2 LOM figure of ~US$41/oz as a long-term projection.

2. **NEM FCF breakeven $1,558/oz**: This specific figure was not found in any primary source reviewed. If this came from an investment deck or analyst note, recommend sourcing the original document. The closest verifiable anchor is NEM's by-product AISC of $1,358/oz (2025) and co-product AISC of $1,566/oz (Q3 2025).

3. **ESG AUM $35T**: Technically accurate for the GSIA 2020 figure; post-methodology change (GSIA 2022) the comparable figure is $30T. Both are defensible in different contexts. Bloomberg Intelligence references $30T as the GSIA-confirmed 2022 figure.

4. **17.8 vs 17.9 years**: Two S&P Global studies produced slightly different numbers (17.9 years in the 2024 study for 2020–2023 mines; 17.8 years in the 2025 study for 2020–2024 mines). Either is accurate with proper attribution to its respective study.

5. **China's Wangu discovery**: The Hunan province Wangu gold field announced November 2024 with estimated 1,000+ metric tonnes (modeled) could potentially qualify as a "major" discovery — but it is not yet fully verified or in production, and S&P Global's methodology requires meeting the 2M+ oz threshold with confirmed reserves/resources. As of S&P Global's 2025 update, it has not been counted among major discoveries per their criteria.
