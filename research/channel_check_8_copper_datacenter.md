# Channel Check 8: Copper & Data Centers — NEM's Embedded AI Infrastructure Option

**Date:** March 31, 2026  
**Ticker:** NEM (NYSE/ASX/TSX)  
**Theme:** Copper exposure as an alternative data channel for valuing Newmont beyond gold models

---

## Executive Summary

The convergence of AI-driven data center construction and a structural copper supply deficit creates a compounding tailwind for Newmont that is not captured in conventional gold-company frameworks. Cadia is not a by-product mine — it is a world-class copper asset that happens to also produce gold. As AI infrastructure spending approaches $389B annually and the global copper market enters its first structural deficit since 2009, NEM's ~150,000 tonne long-term copper production target and 12.5 million tonne copper reserve base represent a call option on the AI buildout that Agnico Eagle and other pure-gold miners simply cannot offer.

**Overall Signal: BULLISH**  
**Confidence: HIGH** on copper demand thesis; **MEDIUM-HIGH** on near-term price magnitude given tariff uncertainty

## Finding 1: AI Data Center Copper Demand

### How Much Copper Per MW of Data Center Capacity?

| Infrastructure Layer | Copper Content | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Power Distribution Systems | 12,000–15,000 kg/MW | BHP, CDA |
| Cooling Infrastructure | 8,000–10,000 kg/MW | BHP, CDA |
| Server Hardware & Interconnects | 4,000–6,000 kg/MW | BHP, CDA |
| **Total (AI hyperscale)** | **27–33 tonnes/MW** | BHP/JPMorgan case study |

The definitive data point: a study of Microsoft's $500M Chicago data center found it used **2,177 tonnes of copper for 80 MW = 27 tonnes/MW**. This is the BHP-cited benchmark and aligns with JPMorgan and CME Group estimates of 27–33 tonnes/MW for AI hyperscale facilities.

For context:
- A conventional enterprise data center uses ~5,000–15,000 tonnes total (5–30 MW range)
- A single hyperscale AI data center can use up to **50,000 tonnes** (per Copper Development Association)
- AI hyperscale facilities use **3–4x more copper per MW** than conventional centers
- China's AI facilities, with double redundancy, run even higher copper intensity of 30–47 t/MW

**Sources:** [BHP (January 2025)](https://www.bhp.com/news/bhp-insights/2025/01/why-ai-tools-and-data-centres-are-driving-copper-demand) | [Copper Development Association](https://www.copper.org/copperconversations/thought-leadership/us-opper-meet-surging-demand-ai-data-centers.php) | [Yahoo Finance / Wood Mackenzie (December 2025)](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/ai-data-centre-buildout-pushes-134755997.html)

### Projected Growth in Global Data Center Capacity (2025–2030)

| Source | 2025 Capacity | 2030 Capacity | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| McKinsey (May 2025) | 82 GW (44 AI + 38 non-AI) | 219 GW (156 AI + 64 non-AI) | 3.5x total; AI grows 3.5x |
| Goldman Sachs (Feb 2025) | ~55 GW | 122 GW | ~2.2x |
| ABI Research (Jan 2026) | 24.4 GW (active IT only) | 147 GW | 6x by 2035 |
| IEA (April 2025) | ~100 GW total | Demand doubles | Power: 415→945 TWh |

Key findings:
- **McKinsey** projects total data center capacity from 82 GW in 2025 to **219 GW by 2030**, with AI workloads growing from 44 GW to 156 GW — a 3.5x increase in AI capacity
- **Goldman Sachs** estimates 50% growth in power demand by 2027 and **165% by 2030** vs. 2023 baseline
- **IEA** (April 2025): Global electricity demand from data centers will **more than double to 945 TWh by 2030** — equivalent to Japan's current total consumption
- US data centers will account for ~50% of all electricity demand growth through 2030; US AI data centers will use more power than all heavy industry combined

**Sources:** [McKinsey (May 2025)](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/week-in-charts/data-center-demands) | [Goldman Sachs (Feb 2025)](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/ai-to-drive-165-increase-in-data-center-power-demand-by-2030) | [IEA via S&P Global Energy (April 2025)](https://www.spglobal.com/energy/en/news-research/latest-news/electric-power/041025-global-data-center-power-demand-to-double-by-2030-on-ai-surge-iea)

### Implied Incremental Copper Demand from AI Data Centers

Using McKinsey's incremental 124 GW of AI-specific capacity to be added between 2025–2030 and 27–33 tonnes/MW copper intensity:

| Scenario | Tonnes Required (2025–2030 cumulative) |
|---|---|
| Low (27 t/MW) | **3.35 million tonnes** |
| Mid (30 t/MW) | **3.72 million tonnes** |
| High (33 t/MW) | **4.09 million tonnes** |

**Annual demand by 2030:**
- **IEA "Energy and AI" (April 2025):** 512 kt/year from direct data center use
- **JPMorgan (November 2025):** 475 kt from data center installations in 2026 alone, up ~110 kt vs. 2025
- **Sprott (including grid upgrades):** ~1,100 kt/year by 2030
- **Forbes/BHP:** Data centers could consume over **500,000 metric tonnes annually by 2030**

This incremental data center copper demand of ~3.5–4.1 Mt through 2030 represents roughly **13–15% of annual global copper production** being absorbed by a single new end market over five years — a demand shock with no historical parallel.

**Signal: BULLISH**  
**Confidence: HIGH** — Multiple authoritative sources (IEA, JPMorgan, McKinsey, BHP) converge on 27–33 t/MW and 2–3% of global copper demand from DCs by 2030. IEA is the most conservative; the actual range skews higher when grid upgrades are included.

## Finding 2: Copper Supply-Demand Balance

### Current Market Balance & Deficit Forecasts (2026–2028)

| Institution | 2025 Balance | 2026 Balance | Source / Date |
|---|---|---|---|
| ICSG (Int'l Copper Study Group) | +178,000 t surplus | **−150,000 t deficit** | [Reuters, Oct 2025](https://www.reuters.com/business/slower-production-growth-will-push-copper-market-deficit-2026-says-icsg-2025-10-08/) |
| J.P. Morgan | Near-balanced | **−330,000 t deficit** | [JPMorgan, Nov 2025](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/commodities/copper-outlook) |
| Wood Mackenzie | Already in deficit | −304,000 t refined deficit | [Yahoo Finance, Dec 2025](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/ai-data-centre-buildout-pushes-134755997.html) |
| Goldman Sachs | +500,000 t surplus (revised up) | Small surplus persists near-term | [Goldman Sachs, Dec 2025](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/copper-prices-forecast-to-decline-from-record-highs-in-2026) |
| S&P Global | — | — | Cumulative 10 Mt deficit by 2040 |

**Key structural drivers of the deficit:**
1. **Mine supply growth decelerating:** ICSG revised 2025 mine production growth to just **1.4%** (from 2.3%). 2026 growth forecast only 2.3%.
2. **Grasberg force majeure:** Grasberg Block Cave (world's 2nd largest copper mine, 70% of site production) suspended due to September 2025 mudslide; expected offline until Q2 2026 — removing critical tonnage
3. **Ore grade decline:** Average copper grades ~40% lower since 1991; exponentially more ore required to maintain output
4. **Project lead times:** 17-year average from discovery to production means **no new supply response is possible before ~2040** for any mine not already in development
5. **Underinvestment:** Industry needs $250B over 10 years to maintain current production; only ~$76B invested in prior comparable period

**Longer-term structural deficit:**
- **S&P Global (January 2026):** Supply deficit reaches **10 million tonnes by 2040** — demand surges to 42 Mt vs. supply of only 32 Mt. This represents a 25% below-demand gap.
- Production **peaks at 33 Mt in 2030**, then declines without massive new investment
- AI/defense demand to **roughly triple by 2040**, adding ~4 Mt combined

**Sources:** [S&P Global Study, Jan 2026](https://press.spglobal.com/2026-01-08-Substantial-Shortfall-in-Copper-Supply-Widens-as-the-Race-for-AI-and-Growing-Defense-Spending-Add-to-Accelerating-Demand,-New-S-P-Global-Study-Finds) | [ICSG via Mining Visuals, Mar 2026](https://www.miningvisuals.com/post/copper-market-balance-a-look-at-2026-deficit-forecasts) | [JPMorgan Copper Outlook, Nov 2025](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/commodities/copper-outlook)

### Is NEM's Copper Exposure a Differentiated Asset?

**vs. Agnico Eagle: YES, clearly differentiated.**  
AEM is a textbook pure-gold company. Its copper exposure is de minimis — Akasaba West produces ~21,000 tonnes total over five years, and Upper Beaver is not yet approved for development. An AEM investor has essentially zero exposure to the AI copper demand thesis. NEM is fundamentally different in this respect.

**vs. Barrick: Partially differentiated, with important nuances.**  
Barrick has higher absolute copper production (~195–230 kt/year) and larger total copper reserves (18 Mt vs. NEM's 12.5 Mt). However:
1. Barrick's copper comes from multiple dispersed assets in riskier jurisdictions (Zambia, Chile, Saudi Arabia, Pakistan/Reko Diq)
2. Cadia is a **single Tier 1 asset** in Australia with operating leverage, existing infrastructure, and multi-decade optionality in a stable, low-political-risk jurisdiction
3. NEM's Cadia copper **costs are extremely low** — the co-product cost structure means copper is effectively subsidized by gold production and vice versa. Copper AISC of $8,510/t at current low-volume phase vs. spot at ~$13,000+/t represents 50%+ margins
4. Red Chris Block Cave (British Columbia) adds another major future copper option — feasibility study expected H2 2026, which could add ~100+ kt/year if approved

**The key differentiation:** NEM offers copper exposure through **the highest-quality, longest-life copper asset in the gold sector** (Cadia), in a Tier 1 jurisdiction, with a massive optionality runway through the 2040s. This is not "copper as a by-product" — Cadia's economics are genuine co-product economics where copper contributes materially to site profitability.

**Signal: BULLISH (differentiated asset vs. gold-only peers)**  
**Confidence: HIGH** vs. Agnico; **MEDIUM-HIGH** vs. Barrick (Barrick is a reasonable copper alternative but different risk profile)

## Synthesis: NEM's Embedded AI Infrastructure Option

### The Core Argument

Gold-only valuation models apply a gold-focused NAV and P/NAV multiple to NEM, then credit copper at some modest assumed price. This misses the following:

1. **Copper is re-pricing structurally.** The 2026 market is entering its first structural deficit since 2009. S&P Global's January 2026 study projects cumulative 10 Mt shortfall by 2040. This is not a cycle — it is a multi-decade supply-demand imbalance.

2. **AI is the demand shock.** McKinsey projects 124 GW of incremental AI data center capacity to be added by 2030, each MW requiring 27–33 tonnes of copper. This alone represents 3.3–4.1 million tonnes of copper demand over five years — from a demand category that effectively did not exist five years ago.

3. **Cadia is at peak optionality.** The timing alignment is exceptional: PC2-3 comes online at full production in 2027 exactly as the copper deficit deepens. PC1-2 reaches peak production in 2030–2040, the period when S&P Global projects the worst supply shortfall.

4. **The option is currently underpriced in models.** NEM's 2026 AISC guidance assumes $5.00/lb copper. Spot prices are ~$6.00/lb. Every $0.50/lb increase in realized copper price translates to approximately **~$75M in incremental pre-tax revenue at current production**, rising to ~$87M+ at the 150 kt long-term target.

5. **Consensus estimates don't capture the copper call.** Analyst models for NEM are primarily gold-price-driven. The forward estimates ($28.6B revenue in FY2026, $29.7B in FY2027) assume roughly flat or modest copper contribution. A structural copper re-rating to $6–7/lb on a 150+ kt production base creates unmodeled EPS upside of $0.30–$0.65/share.

## Risk Factors

1. **Goldman Sachs dissents on 2026:** GS models a 500kt surplus in 2025 persisting into 2026, constraining near-term price to $10,000–$11,000/t range. If their surplus thesis is correct, near-term copper tailwind is muted even as the long-term thesis remains intact.

2. **US tariff timing:** A Section 232 tariff on copper imports (expected mid-2026 per GS base case) creates near-term LME vs. COMEX price divergence and potential demand disruption, though it likely supports long-term incentive pricing.

3. **China demand risk:** China represents ~58% of global refined copper demand. A prolonged Chinese slowdown could meaningfully offset AI-driven demand. ICSG notes Chinese consumption growth decelerating to 2.1% in 2026 from faster prior years.

4. **Cadia geotechnical issues:** NEM's 2025 reserve update showed an 8.5 million oz resource decrease at Cadia partly driven by geotechnical standoff from in-pit tailings facility, plus block model updates. While reserves are still enormous, execution risk at the tailings facility is a watchpoint.

5. **NEM-specific operational execution:** 2026 is NEM's guided production trough (5.3M oz gold). The copper ramp is critical to the thesis; any delay in PC2-3 cave establishment beyond Q4 2026 delays the inflection.

6. **AI efficiency improvements:** DeepSeek and inference optimization could reduce compute intensity per workload, moderating data center build-out relative to hyperscale scenarios.
