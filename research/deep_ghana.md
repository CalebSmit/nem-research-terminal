## 1. NEW ROYALTY LEGISLATION — EXACT DETAILS

### Legislation Name
**Minerals and Mining Royalties (Regulations), 2025**
- This is a Legislative Instrument (L.I.) — a secondary/subordinate piece of legislation enacted under the authority of the existing **Minerals and Mining Act, 2006 (Act 703)**.
- The L.I. was laid before Parliament in December 2025 by the Minister for Lands and Natural Resources, Emmanuel Armah-Kofi Buah.
- The regulation matured into law on **March 9, 2026**, after completing the constitutionally required **21 sitting days in Parliament** (per [Modern Ghana](https://www.modernghana.com/news/1478811/the-cost-of-certainty-what-ghanas-royalties.html)).

## 2. ROYALTY RATE STRUCTURE — EXACT THRESHOLDS

### Previous Rate (Pre-March 2026)
- **Flat rate of 5%** of gross revenues, for large-scale mining operations
- Under Newmont's Ahafo stability agreement: a **sliding royalty of 3%–5%** (rising to 3.6%–5.6% in forest reserve areas) — now expired
- The flat 5% rate was established by **Act 794 in 2010**
- Historical context: Ghana had a sliding scale of 3%–12% from 1986 (linked to mine profitability), then narrowed to 3%–6% in 2006, then collapsed to a flat 5% in 2010

**Sources:**
- Reuters (January 15, 2026 exclusive on Ahafo agreement terms): https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/ghana-scrap-mining-stability-pacts-double-royalties-2026-01-15/
- MyJoyOnline (GHEITI history): https://www.myjoyonline.com/gheiti-clarifies-ghanas-new-mineral-royalty-regime-urges-balanced-approach-to-protect-investment/
- Africa Briefing: https://africabriefing.com/ghana-to-double-gold-royalties-end-stability-deals/

### New Rate — Sliding Scale (Gold)
The new **Minerals and Mining Royalties (Regulations), 2025** establishes a **gold price-linked sliding scale of 5% to 12%**:

| Gold Price (USD/oz) | Royalty Rate |
|---|---|
| Below ~$1,900/oz | 5% (floor) |
| ~$1,900–$2,400/oz | 5% |
| ~$2,400–$2,900/oz | 6% |
| ~$2,900–$3,400/oz | 7% |
| ~$3,400–$3,900/oz | 8% |
| ~$3,900–$4,400/oz | 9% |
| ~$4,400–$4,500/oz | 10–11% (escalating) |
| ≥$4,500/oz | **12% (ceiling)** |

**Mechanism:** Roughly every **US$500 increase in gold prices triggers a 1 percentage point rise** in the royalty rate (per [GHEITI statement, March 13, 2026](https://www.myjoyonline.com/gheiti-clarifies-ghanas-new-mineral-royalty-regime-urges-balanced-approach-to-protect-investment/) and [ferro-alloys.com](https://www.ferro-alloys.com/en/News/Details/332464)).

> **Note:** The exact intermediate band breakpoints (e.g., $2,400 triggers 6%, $2,900 triggers 7%, etc.) are derived from the $500/1% increment rule confirmed by multiple sources. The official L.I. text has not been publicly published in full; the exact boundary points at every increment have not been verified verbatim from the L.I. document itself.

**At current gold prices (~$5,000+ per ounce as of March 2026):** The 12% ceiling rate is already triggered and applicable.

**Structure type:** Sliding scale (NOT flat). Price-linked, adjusted automatically.

### Lithium Royalties (Also Changed)
- New sliding scale: **5%–12%** based on lithium prices from **$1,500 to $3,200 per metric ton**
- Prior rate: Flat 10% (at ~$3,000/t price)

### Other Minerals
- Remain at flat **5%** rate (unchanged)

## 3. WHICH COMPANIES ARE EXEMPT

The following companies **retain their existing development agreement terms** (exempt from the new sliding scale for now):
- **Zijin Mining**
- **AngloGold Ashanti** (agreement lapses ~2027)
- **Gold Fields** (agreement lapses ~2027)

**Newmont's Ahafo stability agreement expired December 31, 2025** and was **not renewed** — confirmed by Minerals Commission CEO Isaac Tandoh. The government denied Newmont's request for extension.

**Therefore, Newmont/Ahafo is SUBJECT to the new sliding scale regime as of January 1, 2026.**

## 4. NEWMONT AHAFO MINE — SPECIFIC IMPACT

### Newmont's Previous Royalty Rate Under Stability Agreement
Under the Ahafo Stability Agreement (original 2006, revised 2015 extension), Newmont paid:
- **Corporate income tax:** 32.5% (vs. standard 35%)
- **Royalty:** sliding scale of **3%–5%** of gross revenues (rising to **3.6%–5.6%** in forest reserve areas)
- Plus duty and VAT relief on qualifying inputs

The stability agreement was a 10-year arrangement, extended with requirements including a minimum $300 million investment threshold and output/mine-life targets.

**Source:** Reuters (January 15, 2026, citing revised 2015 agreement reviewed by Reuters): https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/ghana-scrap-mining-stability-pacts-double-royalties-2026-01-15/
LinkedIn summary (citing same Reuters report): https://www.linkedin.com/posts/syed-salman-shafi_mining-stability-africa-activity-7417793234036248577-rMis

### What Changed When the Agreement Expired (December 31, 2025)
Per Newmont's **Q4 2025 Earnings Release** (filed with SEC on February 19, 2026):
> *"On December 31, 2025, Newmont's 10-year investment stability agreement expired. As a result, revenues from the combined Ahafo South and Ahafo North operations are now subject to the 3 percent Growth and Sustainability Levy. The applicable corporate income tax rate on profits has increased by 2.5 percent to 35 percent. Cash profits repatriated to Newmont are now subject to an 8 percent withholding tax, in addition to the existing one-ninth carried interest royalty."*

**Royalty rate under stability agreement (expired):** 3%–5% (Ahafo-specific sliding scale)
**Royalty rate under standard regime (post-expiry, pending new L.I.):** 5% flat
**Royalty rate under new Minerals and Mining Royalties (Regulations), 2025:** 5%–12% sliding scale linked to gold price (current gold ~$5,000+ = **12% applies**)

**Source:** Newmont Q4 2025 Earnings Release (SEC filing): https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1164727/000116472726000009/newmontq42025earningsand20.htm

### Additional Fiscal Changes at Expiry of Stability Agreement
- **Growth and Sustainability Levy (GSL):** 3% of gross production applied (previously exempt); reduced to **1%** by the Growth and Sustainability Levy (Amendment) Bill, 2026 passed March 14, 2026
- **Corporate income tax:** Increased from 32.5% to **35%** (standard rate)
- **Withholding tax:** 8% on cash profits repatriated (new)
- **One-ninth carried interest royalty:** Already applied, continues

### Newmont's Official Estimate (from Q4 2025 Earnings Release / 2026 Guidance, February 19, 2026)
> *"The Government of Ghana has also proposed a sliding royalty rate of 5 percent to 12 percent dependent on gold price. **If enacted, this royalty change would have a potential impact to Gold AISC of approximately $310 per ounce for our Ghana operations, with a resulting impact for total Newmont of approximately $50 per ounce.** The impact of the sliding scale royalty is not included in 2026 guidance."*

| Impact Metric | Amount |
|---|---|
| AISC impact on **Ghana operations** (Ahafo South + Ahafo North) | **~$310/oz** |
| AISC impact on **total Newmont** | **~$50/oz** |

> **Important caveat:** Newmont uses "$4,500 per ounce" as its gold price assumption in its 2026 cost guidance, but notes gold was trading above $5,000/oz at the time of disclosure. ## 10. GHANA MINERALS COMMISSION / GOVERNMENT STATEMENTS

- **Isaac Tandoh**, CEO, Ghana Minerals Commission: Confirmed the regime takes effect despite opposition from US, China, and Western governments; stated "They met with us; they do not oppose the review in principle" — rejected Western countries' suggestion that 12% should apply only above $5,000/oz.
- **Emmanuel Armah-Kofi Buah**, Minister for Lands and Natural Resources: Laid the L.I. before Parliament December 2025; described it as giving Ghana "the opportunity to capture the benefit in good times."
- **GHEITI** (Ghana Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative): Endorsed the regime as "fair in principle" but warned bands are "overly aggressive and inflexible" (March 13, 2026 statement).
- **Ghana Chamber of Mines CEO Kenneth Ashigbey**: Opposed, called for 4%–8% scale instead; called for GSL to be scrapped altogether; warned the new regime "will diminish new projects and production."
