# Channel Check 6: Competitor Activity
## Alternative Data Channel Check for Newmont (NEM)
**Date:** March 31, 2026  
**Reference Period:** FY 2025 / Q4 2025 Earnings (reported Feb 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

Across all four competitor earnings reports, the dominant theme is **rising AISC** (driven by gold-price-linked royalties, labor inflation, and mine sequencing) alongside **broadly flat-to-declining production** from legacy portfolios. Supply-side structural headwinds — zero major new gold discoveries in 2023–24, average development timelines now exceeding 17–20 years, and global mine production only inching to record highs via incremental gains — are deeply bullish for gold prices and, consequently, for well-positioned incumbents like NEM. Newmont's $1,358/oz FY2025 AISC is the most competitive among major peers on a by-product basis, and its scale and reserves pipeline remain industry-leading.

**Net signal for NEM: BULLISH**, confidence: HIGH (across supply structure), NEUTRAL on near-term production (planned 2026 trough of ~5.3 Moz vs. 5.9 Moz in 2025).

**Signal for NEM:** **STRONGLY BULLISH** — The discovery collapse means no meaningful new gold deposits will enter production this decade that aren't already in development pipelines. This is directly beneficial for NEM's existing 118.2 Moz reserve base (declared year-end 2025) and its 148.7 Moz resource base, which represent irreplaceable in-the-ground assets. Competitors must increasingly pursue M&A (at premium multiples) to replace reserves — NEM has the reserves.  
**Confidence:** HIGH

## Section 7: Overall Channel Check Assessment

### Structural Gold Supply Theme: STRONGLY BULLISH for NEM

The competitor channel check reveals a **structurally constrained supply environment** that benefits existing large-scale, low-cost producers. The key signals:

1. **No new major gold deposits are entering development pipelines.** The 2023–2024 discovery drought is without historical precedent. Given 17–28 year average lead times, this means the current decade's supply is essentially locked in.

2. **All major producers are guiding lower or flat production in 2026 vs. 2025**, despite gold prices above $4,000/oz. This isn't cyclical — it's structural depletion at aging deposits and sequencing through mine plans designed years ago.

3. **AISC inflation is broad-based** (+8–24% range across all peers for 2026), primarily driven by gold-price-linked royalties. This is a **positive quality problem** for producers — higher royalties come with proportionally higher revenue. NEM's by-product credits (copper, silver, zinc, lead) provide a structural cost buffer that peers cannot fully replicate.

4. **NEM's reserve position (118.2 Moz declared gold reserves) is irreplaceable** in the current discovery environment. This reserve base, accumulated over decades, cannot be rebuilt by any competitor at current discovery rates.

5. **World Gold Council confirms structural supply ceiling** — supply growth is likely 0–1% annually in 2026, while demand drivers (ETF investment, central banks, macro uncertainty) remain intact.

### Key Risks (Moderating Factors)

- **NEM 2026 production trough**: The ~10.8% production decline (5.9M oz → 5.26M oz) from planned sequencing is a known/expected development, but creates near-term free cash flow headwind vs. 2025 even at elevated gold prices
- **AISC jump to $1,680/oz (+23.7%)**: Higher than peer guidance percentages; partly mechanical (gold price assumption / royalties) but partly from deferred capex and lower volumes
- **Barrick IPO complication**: NEM's consent obligations on Nevada Gold Mines could distract management or require capital commitment decisions on Fourmile development
- **Yanacocha Sulfides deferral**: Removes a long-dated growth optionality that was part of prior analyst models; signals capital discipline but sacrifices future production upside in Peru

### Bottom Line Signal: **BULLISH** for NEM

The competitor channel check is net positive for Newmont's investment thesis. Structural supply constraints, zero new discoveries, lengthening lead times, and peer-confirmed AISC inflation all support continued high gold prices. NEM's scale, reserve base, by-product credit structure, and completed portfolio rationalization position it favorably versus all major peers. The 2026 production trough is temporary and well-understood by management.
