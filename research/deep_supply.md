## 1. ZERO MAJOR GOLD DISCOVERIES IN 2023 AND 2024

### Verdict: CONFIRMED — Source: S&P Global Commodity Insights

**Primary Source:** S&P Global Commodity Insights, "New finds remain scarce despite gold from major discoveries at 3 Boz," authored by **Paul Manalo**, published **July 29, 2025**.
- URL: https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2025/08/new-finds-remain-scarce-despite-gold-from-major-discoveries-at-3-boz

**Exact quote from S&P Global (July 2025):**
> "Notably, no major discoveries occurred in 2023–24."

**Definition of "major discovery":** A deposit containing at least **2 million ounces (2Moz)** of gold in reserves, resources, and past production.

**Key supporting data from the same S&P Global report:**
- Between 1990 and 2024, **353 deposits** were identified globally containing a total of **3 billion ounces** of gold (reserves + resources + past production).
- Since 2020, only **six** major discoveries have been made, contributing just 27 Moz total in reserves and resources.
- Average size of recent discoveries: **4.4 Moz** for the 2020–2024 period, down sharply from **7.7 Moz** in 2010–2019.
- None of the discoveries made in the **past 10 years** has ranked among the **30 largest gold discoveries** ever recorded.
- Despite higher gold prices, **exploration budgets fell 15% in 2023 and 7% in 2024**, ending the uptrend that began in 2017.

**2024 Annual Report (prior vintage, now superseded):**
S&P Global's 2024 analysis (covering through 2023) identified 350 deposits containing 2.89 Boz — the three additional deposits in the 2025 update (353 total) were "discovered decades ago and only recently met the criteria of having at least 2 Moz." No new 2023 or 2024 discoveries were counted.
- URL: https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/gold-from-major-discoveries-grows-3-although-recent-discoveries-remain-scarce

**Historical context:** The last time the industry found double-digit major gold discoveries in a single year was **2009**. Total major discoveries over the last eight years (2017–2024) are roughly equal to the number found in **2009 alone**, per commentary from analysts citing S&P Global data.

**Note on "first time ever":** Analysts and financial media (citing S&P Global data) describe the 2023–2024 double-zero as "the first time in recorded history" two consecutive years have had zero major gold discoveries. S&P Global's own report language says "notably, no major discoveries occurred in 2023–24" without explicitly using "first time ever," but the data series going back to 1990 has never previously shown two consecutive blank years.

## 2. AVERAGE LEAD TIME FROM DISCOVERY TO PRODUCTION — 17.8 YEARS

### Verdict: CONFIRMED — The 17.8-year figure is precise and current

**Primary Source:** S&P Global Market Intelligence, "From 6 years to 18 years: The increasing trend of mine lead times," authored by **Paul Manalo**, published **April 11, 2025**.
- URL: https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/from-6years-to-18years-the-increasing-trend-of-mine-lead-times

**Exact figure:**
> "The average lead time for mines continues to rise, reaching **17.8 years** for those that became operational between 2020 and 2024."

**This is nearly three times longer** than the lead time for mines that began operations from 1990–1999. Extended periods for exploration, permitting, and financing have significantly contributed to these longer lead times.

**Key data from the same study:**
- Non-operating mines currently undergoing feasibility studies have an estimated lead time of **28 years** — nearly five times the lead time observed in the 1990s.
- Specific example: The **Donlin Gold** project in Alaska was discovered in 1990, expected to begin operations in 2027 — that would be **37 years** from discovery to production.

**Important clarification on related figures:**

| Study | Time Period | Figure | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| S&P Global, April 2025 | Mines operational 2020–2024 | **17.8 years** | Gold + copper + nickel + lithium |
| S&P Global, April 2024 | Mines operational 2020–2023 | **17.9 years** | Same scope |
| S&P Global, June 2023 | 127 mines, 2002–2023 | **15.7 years** average | Precious + base metals |
| S&P Global (gold-specific, 127-mine sample) | 2002–2023 | **15.2 years** | Gold mines only |

**Conclusion:** The 17.8-year figure cited in the gold supply thesis is the **most current and most applicable** figure from S&P Global, covering the most recent cohort of mines (2020–2024). It is not a round number — it is an exact research output from S&P Global Market Intelligence, published April 11, 2025.

**Note:** The figure is sometimes cited as "15-year lag" or "17-year lag" in popular financial media. The precise, sourced, and most current number is **17.8 years** from the April 2025 S&P Global study.

## 5. WORLD GOLD COUNCIL — CENTRAL BANK GOLD PURCHASES

### Verdict: "1,100+ tonnes/yr" claim PARTIALLY ACCURATE — Requires clarification

**Primary Source:** World Gold Council, "Gold Demand Trends Full Year 2024," published **February 5, 2025**.
- URL: https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2024/central-banks
- Summary data URL: https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2024

**Actual figures by year (from WGC primary source):**

| Year | Central Bank Net Purchases (tonnes) | Context |
|---|---|---|
| 2022 | **1,136 t** | All-time record since 1950; highest since suspension of dollar convertibility in 1971 |
| 2023 | **1,050.8 t** | Second-highest year on record |
| 2024 | **1,044.6 t** | Third consecutive year above 1,000t |
| 2010–2021 avg | ~473 t/yr | Historical baseline |

**Exact WGC quote:** "Central banks' insatiable appetite for gold reached a significant milestone in 2024. Having added 712t in the first three quarters of the year, central banks bought a further 333t in Q4 to bring the net annual total to **1,045t**. As a result, they have extended their buying streak to **15 consecutive years**, and, remarkably, 2024 is the third consecutive year in which demand surpassed 1,000t."

**Claim assessment — "1,100+ tonnes/yr":**
- The specific figure "1,100+" as an annual run-rate is **not precise**. The closest data point is 2022's record of **1,136 tonnes**.
- The accurate characterization is that central banks have purchased **over 1,000 tonnes per year for three consecutive years** (2022–2024), averaging approximately **1,077 tonnes/yr** across the three-year period.
- **2025 update:** Per AngloGold Ashanti's 20-F (citing WGC Q4 2025 data), central bank buying in 2025 was **863 tonnes** — a notable slowdown from the 1,000t+ pace, attributed in part to gold's extraordinary price rally. The WGC notes central banks tend to slow purchases when prices rise sharply.

**WGC explanation of the buying trend:**
- Central banks have been net buyers for **15 consecutive years** since the 2008 financial crisis
- Key drivers: De-dollarization, geopolitical uncertainty, desire to diversify reserves away from U.S. Treasuries
- 2024's largest buyers: Poland (+90t), Turkey (+75t), India (+73t), China (+44t reported — actual purchases likely higher)
- Annual purchases of ~1,000t represent approximately **25% of annual mine output**, effectively absorbing one-quarter of all mined gold

**Source:** https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2024

## 6. GLOBAL GOLD MINE PRODUCTION TRENDS

### Verdict: NOT DECLINING (yet) — Flat to marginal growth, approaching plateau

**Primary Source:** World Gold Council, "Gold Demand Trends Full Year 2024," published February 5, 2025.
- URL: https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2024/supply

**Mine production data:**

| Year | Global Mine Production (tonnes) | YoY Change | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2018 | 3,656 t | — | Previous all-time record |
| 2022 | ~3,612 t | ~flat | Stable |
| 2023 | 3,644.1 t | ~+0.9% | Near flat |
| 2024 | 3,661.2 t | ~+0.5% | Tentative new record (pending revisions) |
| 2025 | ~3,700+ t (est.) | +~1% (est.) | WGC Q4 2025 data cited by AngloGold suggests another small increase |

**WGC's own assessment (from February 2025 report):**
> "Although initial estimates suggest that 2024 mine production reached an all-time high of 3,661t, this data is subject to revisions, which make it difficult to state with certainty that the previous record high has been broken."
> "Quarterly data also suggests that recent industry growth trends may be coming to an end. We estimate that Q4'24 mine production of 954t was marginally lower than the 955t reported for Q4'23."

**Long-term view:**
- Metals Focus / WGC (cited in Jan 2026 Tiger Brokers analysis): "Global mined gold production will likely plateau gradually in the coming years, rather than peak and then decline."
- LBMA Alchemist (industry analysis): "In the longer term, a lack of investment in exploration and new projects, alongside an increased prevalence of long lead-time underground projects, will result in a flat to declining profile for global gold mine production."

**Key observation — supply vs. demand math:**
- 2024 total gold demand (including OTC): **4,974 tonnes**
- 2024 mine production: **3,661 tonnes**
- Gap (filled by recycling + producer hedging): ~1,313 tonnes
- Central banks alone absorbed **1,045 tonnes** in 2024 — equivalent to 28.5% of all mine output

**Why output is not declining yet:**
New mine contributions in 2023–2024 include IAMGOLD's Côté mine (Canada), Equinox Gold's Greenstone mine (Canada), and Ghana's Namdini — these offset declines at aging mines in the US (Carlin, Cortez), Australia, and South Africa.

**USGS verification (Mineral Commodity Summaries):**
- 2023 worldwide mine production: ~3,000 metric tons (USGS uses a different accounting method that excludes some secondary production — not directly comparable to WGC's 3,644t figure which uses a broader definition)
- 2024: ~3,300 metric tons per USGS
- Source: https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-gold.pdf

## 7. EXPLORATION SPENDING TRENDS — S&P GLOBAL / WGC DATA

### Verdict: CONFIRMED DECLINE — Gold exploration budgets fell in both 2023 and 2024 despite record gold prices

**Primary Source:** S&P Global Market Intelligence, "CES 2024 — Gold exploration budgets down on value-oriented strategies," published **November 20, 2024** (Corporate Exploration Strategies annual series).
- URL: https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/ces-2024-gold-exploration-budgets-down-on-value-oriented-strategies

**Key findings:**

| Year | Global Gold Exploration Budget | YoY Change | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2022 | ~$6.61B | — | Peak of recent cycle |
| 2023 | $5.91B | **−16%** (−$1.09B) | Eight-year low as gold's share of total exploration |
| 2024 | **$5.55B** | **−7%** | Lowest gold share in a decade (45% of global exploration) |

**From S&P Global (CES 2024 report):**
> "Gold exploration budgets declined 7% to $5.55 billion in 2024, despite high gold prices. The budgets accounted for 45% of global exploration but represented the **lowest share for gold in the past decade**."
> "The number of gold explorers decreased 8% to 1,235 in 2024, influenced by consolidation among major companies and fewer junior explorers."

**The grassroots exploration collapse:**
- Allocations to grassroots-stage (early-discovery) projects fell **16% to $1.08 billion** in 2024 — a record-low 19% share
- Near-mine exploration now accounts for 45% of budgets (shift to de-risked minesite work)
- Junior gold companies saw their budgets decline **21%** in 2024; junior companies' share fell to **33%** — a four-year low
- Intermediate companies: **−13%** year-over-year

**Drilling activity:**
- 2022: 70,008 gold drill holes reported globally
- 2023: ~30,345 gold drill holes — **a 36% drop** year-over-year
- Gold's share of global drill holes fell to a record low of 57% in 2023 (down from prior highs)

**S&P Global World Exploration Trends 2024 (PDAC edition):**
- Global nonferrous exploration budget in 2023: $12.76 billion (−3% YoY)
- Global nonferrous exploration budget in 2024: ~$12.5 billion (−3% YoY)
- Gold exploration fell more steeply than the overall budget, dragging the global number
- Source: https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/world-exploration-trends-2024

**Exploration spending vs. discovery output divergence:**
Per the S&P Global Gold Mining Industry Intelligence page:
- Total gold contained in initial resources declined in 2023 to a **four-year low of 37.1 Moz** — down 7% from 40 Moz in 2022
- Only 30% of new resource announcements in 2023 contained more than 500,000 oz (the average dropped to 0.7 Moz vs. 0.8 Moz in 2022)
- Source: https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/campaigns/gold-mining-industry-intelligence

**The paradox:**
Despite gold prices rising substantially (gold averaged $1,940/oz in 2023 and $2,386/oz in 2024 — per WGC data), exploration budgets fell. The S&P Global explanation: capital market dysfunction for junior miners (elevated interest rates made bond alternatives more attractive), consolidation M&A reducing combined budgets, and company focus on cost efficiency over speculative greenfield exploration.

## 8. DATA QUALITY NOTES AND CAVEATS

### Figures that are verified and exact:
- **$1,358/oz NEM FY2025 AISC** — confirmed from Newmont's primary press release (Feb 19, 2026)
- **17.8-year mine lead time** — confirmed from S&P Global primary research (Paul Manalo, Apr 11, 2025)
- **Zero major gold discoveries in 2023 and 2024** — confirmed from S&P Global primary research (Paul Manalo, Jul 29, 2025)
- **1,044.6 tonnes central bank purchases in 2024** — confirmed from WGC primary report (Feb 5, 2025)
- **3,661 tonnes 2024 global mine production** — confirmed from WGC primary data (Feb 5, 2025)
- **$5.55B gold exploration budget in 2024** — confirmed from S&P Global CES 2024 (Nov 20, 2024)

### Figures requiring nuance:
- **"1,100+ tonnes/yr" central bank purchases** — This is approximately correct as a **three-year average** (2022–2024 averaged ~1,077t), but no single year (except 2022 at 1,136t) hits 1,100+. Most precise claim: "over 1,000 tonnes per year for three consecutive years (2022–2024)."
- **NEM AISC at $1,358/oz vs. peers** — NEM's by-product AISC is the lowest headline among peers but uses a favorable accounting methodology (co-product metals credits). On co-product basis ($1,609/oz), it is comparable to Barrick ($1,637/oz) and above Agnico ($1,339/oz also by-product).
- **Global mine production "declining"** — Not accurate as of 2024. Output is at/near record highs and growing fractionally. The supply constraint thesis rests on the *future* plateau/decline scenario, not a current decline. Long-term outlook from industry forecasters (Metals Focus, WGC, LBMA) is flat-to-eventual-decline, driven by depletion of aging mines and insufficient new discoveries.
