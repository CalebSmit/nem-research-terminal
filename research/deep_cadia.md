## 5. EXACT ALLEGATIONS — CONTAMINATION DETAILS

The Statement of Claim alleges a **"toxic trifecta"** of air, soil, and water contamination causing:
1. Damage to property values
2. Injurious conditions to human health and the environment
3. Ongoing harm to agricultural operations, livestock, and waterways

### Specific Contaminants Alleged

| Contaminant | Details | Where Found |
|---|---|---|
| **Arsenic** | At unsafe levels per lab tests | Private properties, bore water, dams |
| **Lead** | At unsafe levels per lab tests | Drinking water tanks on nearby properties |
| **Chromium** | At unsafe levels | Private properties |
| **Nickel** | At unsafe levels | Private properties |
| **Crystalline Silicon** | From mechanically crushed dust; known to cause harm to human lungs | Airborne dust on properties |
| **PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances)** | "Forever chemicals" including PFOS, PFHxS, PFOA | Belubula River, groundwater, properties |
| **PFOS (specifically)** | At levels 40x the daily intake limit in fish; foam on Belubula River | Belubula River |
| **Heavy metals (general)** | Including those above | Water, soil, blood of residents |

### Sources of Contamination Alleged
1. **Tailings Dam Wall Collapse (2018):** The mine's tailings storage facility wall collapsed in 2018, releasing off-white dust that coated properties for days; triggered ongoing contamination
2. **Mine Dust:** Airborne particulate from the mine site, including from extractor fans above an underground crusher found emitting unfiltered dust at **18 times above the legal limit**
3. **Mine Fluids:** Leakage from the site since approximately **2024**
4. **Tailings Dam:** The dam contains mining waste by-products whose spread and impact on neighboring properties is contested by Newmont

### Temporal Scope of Alleged Contamination
- **Mine dust:** Dating back to approximately **2019**
- **Mine fluids:** From approximately **2024** onward
- **Class period for group membership:** February 2, 2020 to February 3, 2026 (per court orders)

### Relief Sought
1. **Compensation** for property value damage from the "toxic trifecta" of air, surface, and groundwater pollution
2. **Injunction** to restrain Newmont/Cadia Holdings from continuing to pollute

In its July 29, 2025 submission to the NSW Parliamentary PFAS Select Committee, Newmont Cadia stated:

## 9. POTENTIAL FINANCIAL EXPOSURE

### What Has Been Quantified
- **No damages quantum has been publicly disclosed or pleaded** in reported sources.
- Plaintiffs seek compensation (property value damage) and an injunction (operational changes) — but no dollar figure for damages has been reported in any source reviewed.
- The lead plaintiff's lawyer stated: *"Success for us is not about money, it will be measured by the change in operations at Cadia."* — Indicating the injunctive relief may be the primary goal.

### Contextual Figures
- Cadia is Newmont's **single largest asset** by production volume (gold + copper)
- Cadia produces **30+ million tonnes of ore annually** (per ABC News)
- Cadia represents a significant portion of Newmont's $13.5 billion annual Adjusted EBITDA (2025)
- 2026 sustaining capital for Cadia includes "critical tailings facility work" (per Newmont Q4 2025 earnings)

### Comparison: Prior EPA Penalties vs. Class Action Scale
- All prior NSW EPA penalties combined: ~$411,500 (2025 conviction) + ~$350,000 (2023) + ~$325,000 (enforceable undertaking) ≈ **~$1.1 million total regulatory penalties**
- CCSN has stated publicly that a "$325,000 fine for a global corporation that makes tens of millions of dollars each week from one of the largest gold and copper mines in the southern hemisphere is not a genuine deterrent."

**Source:** CCSN website: https://www.ccsn.au/the-class-action
