# Channel Check 4: NEM Regulatory & Permits
**Newmont Corporation (NYSE: NEM) — Alternative Data Channel Check**
**Date Compiled:** March 31, 2026
**Analyst Notes:** All findings sourced from public regulatory filings, government communications, news sources, and SEC disclosures. Signal ratings reflect investment implications only.

---

## Executive Summary

The regulatory landscape for NEM is substantially more complex and adverse in early 2026 than at any point since the Newcrest acquisition in 2023. The most significant development is Ghana's enacted sliding-scale royalty law (effective March 10, 2026), which threatens ~$50/oz to total Newmont AISC — a material number given guidance for ~$1,680/oz gold by-product AISC in 2026. This operates alongside the December 2025 expiration of Newmont's Ahafo stability agreement, adding further Ghana cost layers already in effect. On the positive side, the Lihir Nearshore Barrier received full capital approval in February 2026 and is a structural mine-life positive. Boddington recovered from Christmas bushfire damage by late February 2026. The Cadia class action is at early stages with a 2027+ hearing timeline, but carries long-tail risk. Red Chris block cave permitting is advancing via federal fast-track. The Cadia CCOP (extension to 2050) EIS is still in preparation and has not been filed.

**Net Regulatory Signal: BEARISH (near-term) / NEUTRAL-BULLISH (long-term)**

## Finding 3: Ghana Sliding-Scale Royalty Bill — **ENACTED MARCH 10, 2026** ⚠️ HIGH PRIORITY

### Status: LAW — EFFECTIVE MARCH 10, 2026

This is the most significant adverse regulatory development in this channel check.

### Timeline of Events:
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Dec 2025 | Newmont's 10-year Ahafo investment stability agreement **expires**; Ghanaian government confirms it will **not be renewed** |
| Dec 18, 2025 | Government lays Minerals and Mining Royalty Regulations before Parliament (21-day countdown begins) |
| Jan 15, 2026 | Reuters exclusive: Ghana to scrap all mining stability pacts, double royalties |
| Early Feb 2026 | Finance Minister offers 2-percentage-point reduction in Growth & Sustainability Levy as compromise; Ghana Chamber of Mines still opposes |
| Feb 3, 2026 | After 21-day parliamentary sitting period lapses, legislation takes effect |
| March 9, 2026 | Reuters confirms: regime to take full effect March 10 despite opposition from US, China, Australia, Canada, South Africa, and Western governments |
| **March 10, 2026** | **Sliding-scale royalty ENACTED as law, replacing flat 5% rate** |
| March 13, 2026 | Deutsche Welle: Ghana mining industry shaken; miners warn of investment deterrence |
| March 24, 2026 | Government further amends MIIF Act, slashing sovereign wealth fund's share of royalties from 80% to 2% — transparency concerns raised |

### New Royalty Structure:
- **Previous rate:** Flat 5% (large-scale miners)
- **New rate:** Sliding-scale 5%–12% based on gold price
- **At $4,500/oz:** 12% royalty applies
- **Current gold price:** ~$5,000+/oz → **12% rate currently in effect**
- Lithium royalties also affected (5%–12% based on $1,500–$3,200/tonne price range)

### Impact on Newmont Ahafo:
- **Stability agreement expired:** Newmont's Ahafo stability agreement lapsed December 31, 2025 and **was not renewed** — exposing Ahafo South and Ahafo North to full new fiscal regime immediately
- **Additional fiscal layering already in effect (from Q1 2026):**
  - 3% Growth and Sustainability Levy now applies
  - Corporate income tax increased by 2.5% to 35%
  - 8% withholding tax on repatriated cash profits
  - One-ninth carried interest royalty (ongoing)
- **Sliding royalty impact (per Newmont's own Feb 2026 disclosure):** ~**$310/oz impact to Ghana AISC**; ~**$50/oz impact to total Newmont AISC**
- **Critical note:** This $50/oz total impact is **NOT included in Newmont's 2026 AISC guidance of ~$1,680/oz** — meaning realized AISC could be ~$1,730/oz if royalty fully applied
- **Original estimate in investment thesis:** $30–$50/oz — Newmont's actual disclosed figure of **$50/oz** is at the TOP of this range and confirmed by the company itself

### Diplomatic Opposition (Failed):
- US, China, Australia, South Africa, Canada, UK collectively lobbied Ghana
- Diplomatic missions asked for 12% rate to apply only above $5,000/oz; Ghana rejected
- Ghana's Minerals Commission CEO: "They are not against the review in principle" — government proceeding regardless

## Finding 5: Cadia Class Action — NSW Supreme Court

### Status: Active Litigation, Early Directions Stage

- **Filing Date:** February 2, 2026 (filed by Cadia Community Sustainability Network through William Roberts Lawyers + Aristata litigation funding)
- **Case Name:** RETALLACK v CADIA HOLDINGS PTY LTD (NSW Supreme Court)
- **Plaintiffs:** ~2,000 landowners/residents within 17-km radius of Cadia mine (led by Frances Mary Retallack)
- **Defendant:** Cadia Holdings Pty Ltd (Newmont subsidiary)
- **Claims:**
  - "Toxic trifecta": air (dust), surface water, and groundwater contamination
  - Arsenic, heavy metals (lead, chromium, nickel), PFAS at unsafe levels on private properties
  - Contamination stems from: (1) mine fluids leaking from site since ~2024; (2) mine dust from ~2019 onward
  - Tailings dam wall collapse in 2018 identified as contributing factor
  - Belubula River PFAS contamination (EPA fishing ban in effect)
  - PFOS levels in fish >40x daily intake limit
- **Reliefs Sought:** Compensation for property value damage; injunction to restrain further pollution
- **Litigation Funder:** Aristata Capital (UK-based)
- **First Hearing:** March 17, 2026 (directions hearing before Justice Richard Cavanagh)
- **Next Listing:** July 16, 2026 (directions hearing)
- **Trial Date:** Justice Cavanagh indicated a hearing date **might be established for H2 2027** — no trial date set yet
- **Newmont's Position:** "Defending class action proceedings filed in the Supreme Court of NSW... not appropriate to comment further."
- **Defense Position:** Acknowledged tailings dam wall collapse but contested "implications" and extent of contamination spread; challenged scope of evidence production
- **Prior Regulatory History Supporting Claim:**
  - April 2, 2025: Land & Environment Court ordered Cadia to pay **>$400,000** for three dust emission offences (2021–2023)
  - December 2025: EPA Enforceable Undertaking for 2022 tailings surface air pollution incidents
  - 2024: Company acknowledged mine waste was leaking into groundwater
  - EPA fishing ban on Belubula River (ongoing)
- **NSW Health Finding (2025):** No *definitive* evidence of health impacts from heavy metal exposure; however, EPA water review found elevated heavy metals in groundwater with gaps in monitoring
- **Source:** [Supreme Court NSW — class action page](https://lpab.nsw.gov.au/content/dcj/ctsd/supreme-court/supreme-court-home/cases/class-actions/current-class-actions/cadia-mine-class-action.html); [ABC News March 17, 2026](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-03-17/cadia-goldmine-class-action-nsw-supreme-court/106462950); [AAP News Feb 2, 2026](https://aapnews.aap.com.au/news/farmers-sue-mining-giant-over-alleged-toxic-trifecta)
- **Signal: BEARISH** — Class action now formally before NSW Supreme Court; 2,000 plaintiffs; litigation funded by professional UK firm; prior guilty plea to dust offences and ongoing EPA enforcement strengthen plaintiffs' case. Extended timeline (2027 hearing) means multi-year overhang. Potential liability could run into hundreds of millions of dollars if contamination proven across 2,000 properties in 17km radius. Not currently material to near-term earnings but is a significant tail risk.
- **Confidence:** High

## Finding 7: Boddington Bushfire Recovery (Australia)

## Finding 8: Red Chris Block Cave — Environmental Assessment & Permitting (British Columbia, Canada)

### Status: EA Amendment Review Underway; Feasibility Study Advancing; Federal Fast-Track Added

- **Project:** Transition from open-pit copper-gold mining to underground block cave mining at Red Chris (70% Newmont/NRCML, 30% Imperial Metals/RCDC). Located in Tahltan Territory, NW British Columbia.
- **Production 2025 (100% basis):** 93.1M lbs copper + 92,429 oz gold (exceeded guidance)
- **Production 2026 Guidance (100% basis):** 60–66M lbs copper + 47,500–52,500 oz gold (lower due to mining sequence, stockpile processing, and stripping for next open pit phase)

### Permitting Developments:
1. **November 29, 2024:** Newcrest Red Chris Mining Limited (NRCML) submitted Amendment Application to Environmental Assessment Certificate (EAC) #M05-02 to BC Environmental Assessment Office (EAO). Application moves to 180-day review stage.
2. **March 11–April 10, 2025:** 30-day public comment period completed.
3. **May 9, 2025:** NRCML filed Public Engagement Report with EAO.
4. **February 2025:** BC provincial government added Red Chris expansion to **priority fast-track list** in response to US tariff threats; aims to accelerate critical minerals permitting.
5. **February 2026:** Added to federal Major Projects Office (MPO) fast-track program as a critical minerals project; cited for $60 billion in economic opportunity.
6. **Tahltan Nation Consent:** Consent is **legally required** under BC's Declaration Act. Tahltan Central Government (TCG) conducting parallel Tahltan Risk Assessment (TRA). TCG has expressed frustration with fast-tracking but is engaged in process. **Project CANNOT proceed without Tahltan consent.**
7. **Feasibility Study:** Advancing; completion and FID expected **H2 2026** (per Imperial Metals Feb 19, 2026 announcement).
8. **Environmental Issues:** SkeenaWild (March 2025) published concerns about: (a) mine seepage into salmon-bearing watersheds; (b) BC government fined Red Chris for failing to monitor groundwater; (c) summer 2025 incident: three miners rescued from underground (safety incident during early exploration phase).

### Regulatory Risks:
- Tahltan consent is binary and non-delegable — if TCG Board refuses consent, project cannot proceed
- British Columbia's fast-track intention may create political tension with First Nations consultation obligations
- Water quality/tailings concerns in salmon watersheds remain active issues

- **Source:** [Imperial Metals GlobeNewswire Feb 19, 2026](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/19/3241640/0/en/Imperial-Provides-Update-on-Red-Chris-2025-Production-and-Production-Guidance-for-2026.html); [Tahltan Central Government Red Chris info package Sept 22, 2025](https://tahltan.org/red-chris-block-cave-information-package/); [SkeenaWild report March 2025](https://skeenawild.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/SkeenaWild-Key-Risks-Lessons-Red-Chris-Mine.pdf); [Canadian Mining Journal Feb 16, 2026](https://www.canadianminingjournal.com/featured-article/more-mines-join-federal-fast-tracking-program-part-1/)
- **Signal: NEUTRAL** — Block cave permitting advancing on schedule (H2 2026 FID feasibility completion); federal fast-track a positive. However, Tahltan consent remains a binary, non-waivable risk. Current open-pit operations performing above guidance. Not material to 2026 production.
