## 3. Project Catalyst Restructuring Context

### What Was Project Catalyst?
Project Catalyst was Newmont's post-acquisition cost and productivity overhaul following the **$17 billion acquisition of Newcrest Mining in November 2023**. It was designed to realize **$500 million in annual synergies** within 24 months of deal close.

### Exact Cuts
- **Total positions eliminated: ~3,552** (16% of 22,200 workforce)
- Level of Work 2 (superintendents, leads, specialists): ~12% reduction
- Level of Work 1 (advisors, officers, operators, maintainers): ~10% reduction
- Reductions included job eliminations, unfilled vacancies, and role level adjustments
- Completed **one month ahead of schedule** (announced November 7, 2025)

Sources:
- Reuters, "Newmont's restructuring impacts 16% of jobs," November 7, 2025: https://www.reuters.com/business/newmont-cuts-16-workforce-restructuring-after-newcrest-deal-memo-2025-11-07/
- Discovery Alert, "Newmont Cuts 3,552 Jobs": https://discoveryalert.com.au/workforce-transformation-industry-strategic-restructuring-2025/
- Stocktwits/Reuters wire: https://stocktwits.com/news-articles/markets/equity/newmont-corporation-reduces-global-workforce-by-about-16-report/cL2wGwtREcG

## 4. Newmont Headcount — Then vs. Now (Is It Growing Again?)

### Official Headcount Timeline

| Date | Employees | Change | Source |
|------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Dec 31, 2022 | 14,600 | +200 (+1.4%) | StockAnalysis SEC filings |
| Dec 31, 2023 | 21,700 | +7,100 (+49%) | Newcrest acquisition added ~7k+ |
| Dec 31, 2024 | 22,200 | +500 (+2.3%) | Newmont Q4 2024 earnings release |
| Dec 31, 2025 | **17,500** | **−4,700 (−21%)** | Newmont 2026 DEF 14A proxy statement |

Source (proxy): https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/NEM/def-14a-newmont-corp-de-definitive-proxy-statement-640e053fbf8a.html  
Source (historical): https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/nem/employees/

### Contractor Workforce
| Date | Contractors |
|------|------------|
| Dec 31, 2024 | ~20,400 |
| Dec 31, 2025 | **~26,600** |

**Critical insight:** While direct headcount fell from 22,200 to 17,500 (−4,700 employees, −21%), the **contractor workforce grew substantially from ~20,400 to ~26,600** (+6,200 contractors, +30%). Total combined workforce (employees + contractors) actually **increased** from ~42,600 to **~44,100** year-over-year. This is a common mining industry pattern post-restructuring: direct labor is reduced in favor of contractor workforce for operational flexibility.

### Is Headcount "Growing"? — Assessment

**Not in direct employment terms.** The Project Catalyst cuts were still being executed through 2025, resulting in a net loss of 4,700 direct employees between end-2024 and end-2025. However:

1. **Active graduate recruitment is occurring:** The 2027 Graduate Program (Mine Surveying, Mining Engineering) is actively posting, and the 2026 trainee cohort of 41 students has already started at Cadia, Boddington, and Lihir.
2. **Production-level operational hiring is active:** Boddington (22 open roles) and Tanami (12 open roles) are hiring across trades, maintenance, and engineering.
3. **The contractor base has grown sharply (+30% YoY)**, which may reflect the Ahafo North commissioning ramp-up (commercial production achieved in 2025 per the proxy) and panel cave development activity at Cadia.
4. **2027 is the inflection point for Cadia production growth**, which will likely require material hiring build-up starting in 2026–2027.

**Bottom line:** Newmont is **not growing direct headcount** in early 2026 — the restructuring has driven a significant net reduction. However, it is actively recruiting for specific technical and operational roles, running graduate programs, and growing its contractor base substantially. A broader hiring recovery is more likely to begin in 2026–2027 as Cadia's Panel Cave ramp-up accelerates.
