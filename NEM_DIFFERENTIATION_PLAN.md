## What to Build

Search for NEM's historical guidance and actual results going back as far as data allows (target: 5-10 years, accept whatever is available).

**Sources:**
- NEM's annual 10-K filings (contain full-year actual results)
- NEM's Q4 earnings call each year (management typically gives next-year guidance)
- NEM's investor day or guidance press releases at the start of each year
- Search: "Newmont 2024 guidance," "Newmont 2023 production guidance," etc. for each year
- Sell-side research notes that reference NEM guidance (often contain the exact guided ranges)

**Build this table for each available year:**

| Fiscal Year | Gold Production Guided (Moz) | Gold Production Actual (Moz) | Beat/Miss | AISC Guided ($/oz) | AISC Actual ($/oz) | Beat/Miss | CapEx Guided ($M) | CapEx Actual ($M) | Beat/Miss |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

"Beat" for production means actual > guided (produced more than promised).
"Beat" for AISC means actual < guided (costs came in lower than promised).
"Beat" for CapEx means actual ≤ guided (didn't overspend).

**Then calculate:**
- Production beat rate: X of Y years, NEM beat production guidance
- Average production beat magnitude: +X.X% above guidance on average
- AISC beat rate: X of Y years, AISC came in below guidance
- CapEx discipline: X of Y years, CapEx at or below guidance
- Trend: is the beat rate improving, stable, or declining over time?
- CEO comparison: if enough data spans multiple CEOs, does guidance accuracy differ?

**The punchline:**
NEM has guided FY2026 production at 5.26 Moz and AISC at $1,680/oz (management has stated this is a planned trough year). If the historical pattern shows NEM typically beats guidance by X%, then:
- Expected actual production ≈ 5.26 × (1 + beat rate) = X.XX Moz
- Expected actual AISC ≈ $1,680 × (1 − AISC beat rate) = $X,XXX/oz
- Implied incremental revenue from the production beat alone = X.XX Moz × gold price × beat magnitude
- This incremental revenue flows through to FCF and is NOT in the market's current pricing

Quantify this in dollars. This is the investable insight.

**Peer comparison (if data available):**
Run the same analysis for Barrick Gold and Agnico Eagle. Compare beat rates. If NEM is more conservative than peers, that's additional evidence that the current guidance understates reality.
