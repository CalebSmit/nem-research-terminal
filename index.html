Three Biggest Risks — and Why the Thesis Survives Each
Risk 1 — Gold Price Reversal
Gold falls to $2,800/oz on Fed hawkishness, dollar strengthening, or China slowdown reducing CB demand.
REBUTTAL
Central bank demand is treaty-driven and sovereign, not speculative. 1,100+ t/yr is now a policy-level commitment. Real yields would need to rise 200+ bps simultaneously — implausible with $34T US debt load.
Risk 2 — AISC Blow-Out
FY2026 AISC guidance of $1,680/oz masks underlying inflation — actual AISC breaches $1,900/oz, destroying FCF.
REBUTTAL
The Newcrest synergy program ($500M/yr) is structural, not temporary. Cadia's negative-AISC copper credits create a cost buffer. Even at $1,800/oz AISC and $4,758/oz gold, NEM generates $18+/oz net margin with 6.9 Moz = $5.5B+ FCF.
Risk 3 — Dilutive M&A
Management reverts to form and executes a $10B+ acquisition at premium multiples, destroying per-share value as in 2019–2023.
REBUTTAL
Post-Newcrest, the Board has a zero-tolerance M&A policy for 2026. Tom Palmer explicitly committed to buyback focus. At P/NAV of ~1.0×, buybacks are clearly value-accretive vs. acquisition of any peer at 1.3–2.8× NAV.
